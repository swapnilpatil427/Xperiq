## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP — The Mid-Market Insights Team

**Company profile:**
- 500–5,000 employees
- Industries: B2B SaaS, Financial Services (banking/insurance), Healthcare (payer/provider),
  Retail/eCommerce, Professional Services
- Has an active survey program (already uses Experient or a competitor for NPS/CSAT)
- Has 1–5 dedicated research or insights staff — not a full research organization,
  but more than a single analyst

**Buyer persona — "The Research Lead":**
- Title: VP Research, CX Insights Manager, Senior UX Research Lead, Director of Customer Intelligence
- Age: 32–48
- Education: Masters in social science, psychology, or UX design
- Manages: 1–3 direct reports, $25k–$150k annual research tool budget
- Key frustration: "I can tell my leadership what our NPS score is. I cannot tell them why
  it dropped last quarter without spending $40k and waiting 8 weeks."
- Reads: Greenbook, Quirks, ResearchWorld, Nielsen Norman Group, UX Collective
- Attends: ESOMAR, Greenbook IIEX, UXPA, EPIC conference
- Evaluates tools by: talking to peers, reading practitioner reviews on G2, watching
  webinar demos before booking a sales call

**Economic buyer persona — "The CX Executive":**
- Title: VP Customer Experience, Chief Customer Officer, VP People & Culture (for EX)
- Approves budget based on: "How fast will we understand why NPS dropped?"
- Cares about: time-to-insight, not methodology; board-ready outputs, not codebooks
- Sign-off threshold: typically $25k–$100k/year for research tools at this company size

**Champion persona — "The Working Researcher":**
- Title: UX Researcher, Customer Insights Analyst, Market Research Manager
- This is the person who actually runs studies
- She will veto a tool that is clunky, slow, or produces outputs she can't defend
  to her manager
- She needs: something that fits into her sprint cycle; AI analysis she can review
  and edit (not a black box); exports she can paste into the deck today

### Secondary ICP — The Enterprise Research Function

**Company profile:**
- 5,000+ employees
- Has a formal market research or CX analytics function (5–20 researchers)
- Already spending $100k+ on Qualtrics or Medallia for quant
- Running qual through services firms (Ipsos, Kantar) at $50k+ per study

**Opportunity:** They want to bring qual in-house with AI, reduce reliance on agency
costs, and connect qual directly to their existing quant program.

**Sales motion:** Enterprise sales-led, not self-serve. VP Sales + Solutions Consultant
run custom demos with live proof-of-concept studies.

### Negative ICP (Do Not Target)

- Companies with no research function (no champion, no budget, no ROI)
- Companies that only run NPS and don't care about understanding it
- Regulated industries where research requires IRB approval for every study
  (clinical research, pharmaceutical) — our ethics framework is not designed for that
- Companies with <100 survey respondents in their database (recruitment foundation too thin)

## 2. Positioning

### The Category We Are Creating

We are not entering the "qualitative research tools" category. We are creating
**"connected experience intelligence"** — the category where quant signals and qual
understanding live in the same platform, analyzed by the same AI, acted on in the
same place.

This matters because:
- Positioning against "another qual tool" forces price competition with UserTesting
  and dscout, who have 10-year head starts
- Positioning as "the platform that closes the qual-quant loop" is defensible and true,
  and no competitor can claim it because it requires owning both sides of the data

### Positioning Statement

> For CX and research leaders at mid-market companies who need to understand not just
> what their customers feel but why — Experient Human Qualitative Panels is the first
> research platform where your qualitative findings live in the same place as your
> survey data, analyzed by AI that already knows your metrics. Unlike standalone qual
> tools that produce PowerPoint decks that nobody reads, Experient creates a closed loop:
> quant signals trigger qual investigations, qual themes connect back to your metrics,
> and Crystal proposes the next action — so every insight drives the next decision.

### Messaging Framework

**Primary message (for all audiences):**
"Your NPS tells you the score. We help you understand the story behind it."

**For the Research Lead:**
"Run a real qualitative study in an afternoon, not 8 weeks. Recruit from your own
respondents, launch a 5-day async study, and get AI-coded themes reviewed and
ready to present by the next morning."

**For the CX Executive:**
"Stop spending $40k and 8 weeks to answer 'why did NPS drop?' Start getting that
answer in 48 hours, from the actual customers who scored you low."

**For the Working Researcher:**
"The AI does the transcription and the first pass at themes. You review, edit, and
approve. You spend your time on insight, not on reading 40-page transcripts."

**Against UserTesting:**
"UserTesting is a UX research tool. Experient connects qual to your CX and EX
metrics — your NPS, your CSAT, your employee engagement — so every qual study
answers a question your metrics already raised."

**Against dscout:**
"dscout is a diary study platform. Experient gives you the full qual toolkit —
async communities, video interviews, diary studies — with AI analysis and a direct
connection to the quant data you already have."

**Against Dovetail:**
"Dovetail is a repository for research you already ran elsewhere. Experient is
where you run the research, analyze it, and connect it to your metrics. One place,
one workflow, one platform."

**Against Qualtrics qual:**
"Qualtrics qual requires a services engagement and a $150k+ contract. Experient
is self-serve, credit-based, and available today. And it connects to your existing
survey data natively — because it's the same platform."

## 5. Launch Phases

### Phase 1 — Private Beta with Design Partners (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** 10–15 companies running real studies on the platform before public launch.
Not demos — real qual studies with real participants producing real insights.

**Who we recruit as design partners:**
- Existing Experient customers with active survey programs (warm relationship)
- 2–3 companies recommended by the CRO and Principal Qual Methodologist from their
  professional networks (credibility signal)
- 1–2 research agencies who want an AI-native platform for their client work
  (they become reseller advocates)

**What design partners get:**
- Free access during beta (no credits consumed)
- Direct access to the CRO and Principal Qual Methodologist for methodology guidance
- First input on the product roadmap
- Named case study rights (their team featured in launch content with approval)

**What we get:**
- Real usage data on where the product breaks
- 3–5 publishable case studies ready for the public launch
- Testimonials from real researchers (not just "it's great" but "here's the specific
  time-to-insight improvement")
- The CRO gets enough methodology evidence to write a credible white paper

**Design partner success criteria:**
- Each partner runs at least 2 studies during the beta period
- At least 8 of 15 partners upgrade to a paid plan at launch
- At least 3 publishable case studies approved and ready

**The launch moment:**

The CRO delivers a keynote or featured session at Greenbook IIEX or ESOMAR Congress
titled: *"The End of the 8-Week Qual Study: AI, Async Research, and the Future of
Experience Intelligence."*

This is not a product launch talk. It is a methodology paper delivered by a credible
researcher who happens to work at Experient. The product is the proof, not the pitch.

**Launch week activities:**

Monday: CRO publishes white paper on AI-assisted qualitative research methodology

          developer credibility)

Wednesday: CRO keynote or keynote + press coverage in Quirks, Greenbook, Research Live

Thursday: Webinar: "How [Design Partner Name] cut qual time from 6 weeks to 48 hours"
           Live demo with real study from a design partner

Friday: Email blast to entire Experient user base: "Qual research is now built into
         your Experient account. Here are 500 free credits to run your first study."

**Launch metrics (30 days):**
- 500 trial accounts activated
- 150 studies launched
- 75 AI theme briefs generated
- 25 paid conversions (Starter or Growth)
- 10 enterprise pipeline opportunities opened

## 6. Channel Strategy

### Channel 1 — Content and Thought Leadership (owned)

**Owner:** Head of Content + CRO

The research community acquires tools through peer recommendation and content
credibility, not through ads. This is the highest-ROI channel and the hardest
to build — which means it is the most defensible once built.

**Content engine:**

*Monthly flagship content:*
- 1 deep methodology article (CRO or Principal Qual Methodologist authored):
  e.g., "How to design a 5-day async community that actually produces valid themes,"
  "The AI thematic coding problem: what works, what doesn't, and how to know the
  difference," "When qual research is required, when it isn't, and how AI changes that calculus"

*Bi-weekly practitioner content:*
- How-to guides: "Run your first async study in 45 minutes"
- Templates: downloadable discussion guide templates for 6 common study types
- Customer stories: "How [Company] cut their churn investigation time from 6 weeks to 3 days"

*Quarterly thought leadership:*
- White papers (co-authored by CRO and a recognized industry researcher)
- Webinar series: "AI and the future of qualitative research" — 4-part series
  with external practitioners as guests (not Experient employees)

**SEO strategy:**
- Target: "qualitative research software," "online research community platform,"
  "AI thematic analysis," "async research tool," "qual research platform"
- Pillar pages: comprehensive guides to each research method that Experient supports
- Answer-the-question content: "how long does qualitative research take,"
  "qualitative vs quantitative research," "thematic analysis guide"

**Owner:** Head of Product + VP Marketing + Demand Gen Manager

Every existing Experient customer is a potential qual panel customer. The PLG motion:

1. **In-product prompt:** When Crystal detects a quant anomaly, it suggests a qual

   awareness with every shared deliverable.

## 7. Sales Playbook

### Qualification Framework (MEDDIC-R)

| Letter | What to Identify |
|---|---|
| **M** — Metrics | What is their current time-to-qual-insight? What does a qual study currently cost them? |
| **E** — Economic Buyer | Who approves the $25k–$100k research tool budget? |
| **D** — Decision Criteria | How will they evaluate? (Usually: methodology credibility, ease of use, AI quality, pricing) |
| **D** — Decision Process | Who else is involved? Legal (DPA), IT (SSO), Research team lead (methodology) |
| **I** — Identify Pain | "We can't do qual at the pace we need" vs. "We want to replace agency spend" vs. "We need to connect our survey data to qualitative insight" |
| **C** — Champion | Is the Research Lead (the practitioner) championing this internally? |
| **R** — Research | Have they evaluated UserTesting, dscout, Dovetail? Where did those fail? |

### Discovery Questions (High-Signal)

1. "Walk me through the last qual study you ran. How long did it take from brief to insight?"
2. "What happened to the results? Where do they live today?"
3. "When your NPS dropped last quarter, how did you find out why?"
4. "How much did your last focus group or IDI program cost, all-in?"
5. "If you could have the answer to one qual question this week — what would it be?"
6. "Who sees your research output? What format do they want it in?"

The answers to questions 1 and 3 almost always close the value case by themselves.

### Demo Flow (45 minutes)

**Minutes 1–5: Discovery framing**
Recap what you learned in discovery. "You told me the last qual study took 6 weeks
and cost $35k. Today I want to show you what that study would look like if it took
48 hours and cost $200."

**Minutes 5–20: Live study launch**
Open Experient. Filter the respondent database to the target segment
(use their actual customer profile — you prepped this). Show the activity builder.
Launch a study with 3 activities. Show the participant invitation.
If possible, have 3 internal Experient employees waiting as "participants" who respond
in real time during the demo.

**Minutes 20–35: AI analysis**
Because internal "participants" already responded before the demo, jump to a pre-loaded
study with 20 responses. Run the AI theme brief live. Watch Crystal propose themes.
Show the researcher review workflow. Show the executive brief export.
Run a Crystal qual query: "What did participants say about pricing?" — instant answer.

**Minutes 35–42: The Crystal bridge**
Show the quant-qual connection: "Here is your NPS dashboard. Here is where Crystal
proposed an investigation when NPS dropped. Here is how I would click through from
the NPS chart to launching this study in 60 seconds."

**Minutes 42–45: Commercial and next steps**
"A 20-person study like this would cost about 1,100 credits — roughly $11 at list
price. Most of our Growth customers run 3–4 studies per month for $2,000/month total.
Want me to set up a proof-of-concept study on a real question your team has right now?"

### Commercial Objection Handling

**"We already use UserTesting."**
"UserTesting is a great UX research tool. This is different — you're using it to
understand your product interface. We're talking about understanding why your NPS
dropped, why churn is up in your enterprise segment, why employee engagement fell
after the reorg. Those are CX and EX questions. Does UserTesting connect those
findings back to your NPS or CSAT data?"

**"We have Qualtrics — doesn't it do this?"**
"Qualtrics has qual capabilities, but they're services-led — each study requires
working with their professional services team and costs $25k+. We're self-serve.
You can launch a study in an afternoon without talking to anyone. And we're the only
platform that automatically connects the qual findings back to your existing survey
metrics in Crystal."

**"I'm not sure our researchers will trust AI-coded themes."**
"That's exactly why we built it as researcher-in-loop, not AI-only. Crystal proposes
themes with supporting quotes for each. Your researcher reviews every theme, merges,
renames, or rejects them. Nothing ships to your stakeholder deck without human approval.
The AI handles the 15-hour manual coding job; the researcher handles the 2-hour
judgment job. Would you like to see the review workflow in the demo?"

**"The price is too high."**
"What are you spending on agency-run qual studies today? ... So one agency focus
group pays for our Growth plan for a year. And the next one is free. That's the math."

## 8. Customer Success Motion

### Onboarding — The "First Study in 48 Hours" Goal

Every new customer should complete their first real qual study within 48 hours of
account activation. The CSM owns this.

**Onboarding sequence:**
- Hour 0: Welcome email with 3 links: quickstart video (4 min), template library,
  book a setup call with CSM
- Hour 4: Automated nudge if study not yet started: "Most customers run their first
  study within 24 hours. Here's a template for your first async study."
- Day 1: CSM setup call (30 min): understand their immediate research question, help
  them choose a template and set up their first study
- Day 2: First study launched. CSM checks participant response rate at 24 hours.
- Day 3: AI theme brief generated. CSM reviews with customer: "What did you learn?
  How does this connect to what you already knew from your surveys?"
- Day 7: First debrief: "Here are the 3 things we'd do differently on your next study."

**Success metric:** First study completed within 7 days of account activation.
Customers who complete a study in week 1 retain at 3x the rate of those who don't.

### Expansion Triggers

| Signal | CSM Action |
|---|---|
| 3+ studies/month consistently | Offer Growth upgrade for unlimited studies |
| Study participant count approaching limit | Show cost comparison: per-credit vs. subscription |
| Crystal makes quant-qual proposal that customer dismisses | Ask why — often the bridge isn't set up correctly |
| Researcher shares results with executive | "Would it be useful to have the executive automatically get this brief?" → sell stakeholder sharing feature |
| Multiple researchers in the same org using separate accounts | Consolidate to team plan, introduce RBAC |

## 10. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Enterprise research buyers don't trust AI-coded themes | High | High | CRO credibility, researcher-in-loop design, white paper with validation methodology. This is THE core risk — we address it in every customer touchpoint. |
| Participant response rates are lower than projected | Medium | High | A/B test invitation copy from day 1; optimize incentive levels; behavioral scientist advises on engagement design |
| Competitor (UserTesting, Dovetail) launches a quant-qual bridge | Low-Medium | High | Our moat is that we own the quant side (survey + NPS data). They'd need to acquire a survey platform to replicate this. Speed matters — ship Phase HQ-4 on schedule. |
| AI thematic coding regressions damage customer trust | Medium | High | AI Evaluation Engineer owns an automated eval harness; regression detection before any model update ships to production |
| Design partners don't convert to paid at launch | Medium | Medium | Ensure design partners are running real studies with real business value, not toy cases. CSM owns conversion from beta to paid. |
| Sales cycle longer than projected at enterprise | Medium | Medium | Solutions Consultant runs POC studies during the sales cycle — the value becomes undeniable when the prospect sees their own customers' themes |
