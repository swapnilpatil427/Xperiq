# Xperiq Sales Playbook

**Version 1.0 — June 2026**
**Owner: Sales Team**

---

## The Sales Philosophy

Xperiq sells through demonstration, not description. The most powerful sales moment is when a prospect watches Crystal AI answer a question about their own data in real time.

**The golden rule:** Get to the demo as fast as possible. Everything before the demo is qualification. Everything after is deal management.

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

The fastest, easiest customers to win look like this:

| | ICP Criteria |
|--|--------------|
| **Company size** | 50–5,000 employees |
| **Industry** | Tech, Professional Services, Financial Services, Healthcare, Retail |
| **Budget signal** | Already paying for surveys (SurveyMonkey/Typeform/Qualtrics) |
| **Urgency signal** | Recent NPS drop, new CX hire, product launch, or failed Qualtrics renewal |
| **Decision speed** | Can buy in 2–6 weeks (not 6-month procurement cycles) |
| **Data maturity** | Runs at least 2 surveys per year and reviews the results |

### Red flags — deprioritise these leads
- Solo operator / no team — too small, no budget
- Government / public sector — procurement is 6+ months
- No existing survey program — they are not ready
- Wants extensive custom development before paying — wrong fit

## 2. Buyer Personas

### Persona 1: "Constance the CX Director"

**Who she is:** VP or Director of Customer Experience at a 300-person B2B software company. Reports to the COO. Runs NPS programs, customer interviews, and product feedback surveys. Measures success by NPS score and churn rate.

**Her goals:**
- Reduce churn by identifying early warning signs in customer feedback
- Prove the ROI of her CX program to leadership
- Stop spending 3 days a week manually analysing survey data

**Her fears:**
- Buying another tool that doesn't get adopted
- Looking bad when leadership asks what the CX program is producing
- Being compared to Qualtrics and losing on feature checklist

**Her dream scenario:**
> "I run a survey, Crystal analyses it overnight, and I walk into Monday's leadership meeting with a 3-bullet answer to 'what are customers saying?' — without anyone doing a data pull."

**What to show her in a demo:**
Crystal answering "Why did our NPS drop this quarter?" — with cited themes, specific responses, and a recommended action.

---

### Persona 2: "Marcus the HR Director"

**Who he is:** Director of People at a 600-person professional services firm. Runs engagement surveys twice a year. Shares results with executives. Wants to show that HR is data-driven.

**His goals:**
- Understand what's driving attrition before people resign
- Turn engagement survey data into actionable people programmes
- Make the exec team trust the HR data

**His fears:**
- Survey results that say "morale is good" when the company is losing people
- Presenting flat survey charts with no clear next step
- Spending $80k on Qualtrics and still doing analysis in Excel

**His dream scenario:**
> "After the all-hands survey closes, Crystal gives me a report: top drivers of disengagement, which teams are at risk, and what three things we should do about it. I present that to the CEO."

**What to show him in a demo:**
Crystal running on employee survey data — identifying themes by department, flagging at-risk signals, and proposing action items.

---

### Persona 3: "Priya the Startup Founder"

**Who she is:** CEO/Founder of a 30-person SaaS startup. Does customer success herself. Runs ad hoc surveys whenever a customer churns or a major feature ships. Has no dedicated CX analyst.

**Her goals:**
- Understand why customers churn without hiring a researcher
- Make product decisions based on real feedback, not gut feel
- Do it in minutes, not days

**Her fears:**
- Paying for a tool that requires setup time she doesn't have
- Enterprise products that assume she has a 10-person data team
- Paying for Qualtrics when she can't even afford a full-time CX hire

**Her dream scenario:**
> "I upload my Typeform export, Crystal analyses it in 60 seconds, and tells me the top 3 things my customers are saying. That's it. That's all I need."

**What to show her in a demo:**
Upload → Crystal analyses → insight summary. Speed is everything. Show it in under 5 minutes.

## 3. The Sales Motion

### Step 1: Qualify (5–10 minutes, phone or email)

Before a demo, confirm:
- [ ] Do they currently run surveys? (Yes → proceed)
- [ ] How do they currently analyse results? (Manual/Excel → huge pain = good prospect)
- [ ] Who needs to be involved in the buying decision?
- [ ] What is the timeline? (Actively looking now, or exploring?)

Use this script for a discovery email:
> Hi [Name],
>
> I saw you're running [NPS/employee surveys/customer feedback] at [Company]. We built Xperiq specifically for teams who collect a lot of feedback but struggle to turn it into clear answers fast.
>
> Crystal, our AI, can answer "Why did NPS drop?" or "What's driving attrition?" in about 60 seconds — from your existing survey data.
>
> Would it be worth a 20-minute look? I can show you a live demo with your actual data if you can share a sample.
>
> [Your name]

---

### Step 2: Book the demo

Goal: get them on a 30-minute video call.
Tools: Calendly (free) — share your booking link in every email.

Frame the meeting:
> "30 minutes — I'll show you Crystal AI on data similar to what you're running. If it clicks, we'll talk about next steps. If it doesn't, you've lost 30 minutes."

---

### Step 3: Run the demo (5-step structure)

**Total time: 25–30 minutes**

| Step | Duration | What to do |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 1. Set the stage | 3 min | "Before I show you anything — what's the one question you most wish your survey data could answer?" Write it down. |
| 2. Show the problem (brief) | 2 min | Show a typical survey data export — a sea of numbers. "This is what most teams are working with." |
| 3. Crystal live demo | 10 min | Type their question into Crystal. Let it run. Talk through the answer, the cited themes, the confidence score. |
| 4. Show 3 more features | 7 min | Insight layers (descriptive → prescriptive), workflow triggers, report export |
| 5. Pricing + next step | 5 min | "Here's how it's priced. Does it make sense to try it with your actual data?" |

**Demo rules:**
- Use realistic dummy data for the demo — never show another customer's real data
- Let Crystal's answer speak. Do not interrupt while it's generating.
- If they ask a question Crystal can't answer perfectly, that's fine — say "this is where it's still maturing, here's the roadmap."
- End with a specific next step: "Can we set up a pilot for 30 days?" not "Let me know if you have questions."

---

### Step 4: Send a proposal

Send within 24 hours of the demo. Keep it short.

**Proposal structure (1 page):**
1. Problem (what they told you in the call)
2. Solution (what Xperiq does for them specifically)
3. Pricing tier recommended + why
4. Pilot offer (30-day trial or proof-of-concept)
5. Next step (sign here / reply to this email)

**Do not:** Send a 15-page PDF. It gets ignored.
**Do:** Use a Google Doc or Notion page they can comment on.

---

### Step 5: Close

Follow up 3 times over 2 weeks if no response:
- Day 2: "Any questions on the proposal?"
- Day 7: "Happy to do a second session with [other stakeholder] if that helps."
- Day 14: "Following up one last time — if timing isn't right, no problem. When would be a better moment?"

After 3 follow-ups with no response: move to "nurture" in CRM. Check back in 60 days.

## 4. Pricing Guidance

(Note: adjust these tiers to match your actual pricing in the product)

| Tier | Who it's for | What to say |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Starter** | Small teams, startups | "Everything you need to get started — no setup required, instant Crystal access." |
| **Growth** | Mid-market teams | "For teams running multiple surveys with multiple stakeholders — Crystal at full power." |
| **Enterprise** | Large orgs | "Custom contracts, SSO, dedicated success manager, SLA." |

### Pricing objection handling

**"It's too expensive."**
> "Compared to what? If your analyst spends 2 days per survey on analysis, and you run 6 surveys a year, that's 12 analyst-days. At a fully-loaded cost of $400/day, that's $4,800 in analysis cost per year — before you account for delayed decisions. Xperiq costs less and gives you the answers in minutes."

**"We're already using Qualtrics."**
> "Then you're already investing in experience management — which means you get the value of this category. The question is whether Qualtrics's AI is giving you the same depth as Crystal. Most teams tell us they're still doing the analysis in Excel after Qualtrics collects the data."

**"We need to talk to IT / legal / procurement."**
> "Totally understood. Can I send you a security overview and standard data processing agreement? That covers 95% of what IT and legal need. And if your procurement team needs a vendor questionnaire, I can turn that around in 24 hours."

**"Can you do a free trial?"**
> "Yes — 30-day pilot on us. You give me one real survey, I'll have Crystal analyse it and show you the output. If it's not better than what you're doing now, you walk away with nothing lost."

**"We don't have budget this quarter."**
> "No problem — when is your next budget cycle? I'll set a reminder and reach out then. In the meantime, would it be useful to build an internal business case? I can give you an ROI calculator."

## 5. CRM Setup (HubSpot Free)

### Pipeline stages

| Stage | Definition | Goal |
|-------|-----------|------|
| **Lead** | Contact added, not yet qualified | Book a discovery call |
| **Qualified** | Confirmed pain, budget signal, right role | Book a demo |
| **Demo Done** | Demo delivered, interest confirmed | Send proposal in 24h |
| **Proposal Sent** | Proposal delivered | Follow up, answer objections |
| **Negotiating** | Verbal yes, working on terms/contract | Close in 7 days |
| **Closed Won** | Contract signed, payment received | Hand off to onboarding |
| **Closed Lost** | No deal, documented reason | Re-engage in 60–90 days |

### What to track for every deal
- Company name, contact name, title
- ICP score (high/medium/low)
- Source (LinkedIn, cold email, referral, inbound)
- Next action + due date (never leave a deal without a next step)
- Close date estimate
- Deal value (MRR)
- Loss reason (if lost)

## 6. Referral Program

Once you have 10 paying customers, launch a simple referral program:

> "Refer a company to Xperiq. If they become a customer, you get one free month of your subscription."

This is the most cost-efficient source of new customers. Set it up in HubSpot and email your entire customer list.
