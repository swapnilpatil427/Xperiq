## Platform 1: LinkedIn (Primary Channel)

### Why LinkedIn first
Your buyers (CX Directors, HR VPs, Operations leaders) are on LinkedIn daily. Organic reach here is higher than any other B2B platform. It is the highest-ROI channel at zero cost.

### Account strategy
- **Founder personal page** — post 3–5×/week (personal posts outperform company pages 10:1 at early stage)
- **Company page** — post 3×/week (repurpose and supplement founder content)

### What to post

**Format 1: Text posts (highest reach on LinkedIn)**
- 3–5 short paragraphs
- No links in the post body (LinkedIn suppresses link posts) — add link in first comment
- Hook in the first line: "The reason most CX programs fail isn't data. It's this."
- End with a question to drive comments

**Format 2: Carousels (highest saves and shares)**
- 7–10 slides
- Slide 1: Bold claim or question
- Slides 2–8: One insight per slide
- Slide 9: Summary
- Slide 10: CTA + logo
- Topic ideas: "5 things Crystal found in 100k survey responses", "The 4 insight layers every XM program needs"

**Format 3: Video (highest impressions)**
- 60–90 seconds max for native LinkedIn video
- Caption everything (80% watch with sound off)
- Crystal AI demo videos perform extremely well: raw screen recording, no polish needed
- Hook in first 3 seconds: show the result before the process

**Format 4: Polls**
- Run 1 poll per month
- Keep it specific: "What do you do with survey data after collecting it?" (a) Share a dashboard (b) Run manual analysis (c) Export to Excel (d) It sits there
- Reply to every comment

### Posting schedule (LinkedIn)
| Day | Content type | Pillar |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Monday | Text post (thought leadership) | Experience Intelligence |
| Wednesday | Carousel or video | Crystal demo |
| Friday | Text post or short story | Customer story or company update |

### LinkedIn hashtags to use (add to every post)
`#experiencemanagement` `#customerexperience` `#AIanalytics` `#xperiq` `#crystalai` `#CXleadership` `#HRtech` `#surveytips`

### Engagement rules
- Reply to every comment within 24 hours — this boosts algorithmic distribution
- Comment on 3–5 posts by XM/CX influencers per day (adds to personal brand visibility)
- Connect with 10–20 target buyers per week (personalised connection note)

## Platform 2: X / Twitter

### Why X
Journalists, tech founders, and the XM/CX community are active here. Less direct revenue than LinkedIn but strong for press attention, community presence, and fast-feedback testing of messages.

### What to post

**Format 1: Short-form insights (1–3 sentences)**
> "Most survey platforms give you a number. Xperiq gives you a reason.
> Ask Crystal why your NPS dropped and it cites specific themes, in seconds.
> That's the difference between data and intelligence."

**Format 2: Crystal demo GIFs**
Record a 10–15 second screen capture of Crystal answering a question. Post with caption:
> "I asked Crystal: 'What's driving churn in our product feedback?' Here's what it found."
These perform exceptionally well on X.

**Format 3: Repurposed LinkedIn content (shortened)**
Take your LinkedIn text post and cut it to the first 2 paragraphs. Post on X.

**Format 4: Replies and community engagement**
Spend 15 minutes per day replying to conversations about: AI, XM, CX, HR tech, survey tools.
Do not pitch. Contribute. Be the smartest person in the thread.

**Format 5: Thread (1× per week)**
5–8 tweets expanding on one topic:
> "Why most companies have terrible experience data (a thread)"

### Posting schedule (X)
- 5 posts per day (can batch-schedule in Buffer)
- Morning: 1 insight or demo
- Midday: 1 repurposed LinkedIn post or reply burst
- Evening: 1 question or poll

### Engagement rules
- Follow: XM practitioners, CX analysts, HR leaders, SaaS founders, tech journalists
- Never auto-DM — it destroys trust
- Retweet with comment (not just RT) to add your perspective

## Platform 3: YouTube

### Why YouTube
YouTube is the second largest search engine. Demo videos and thought leadership here get found organically for years. One good Crystal demo video can drive signups months after posting.

### Content types

**Type 1: Crystal AI demos (produce 2× per month)**
- Real data, real questions, real Crystal answers
- Length: 3–8 minutes
- Format: screen recording + voiceover, no webcam needed
- Example: "I gave Crystal 2,000 employee survey responses. Here's what it found in 60 seconds."

**Type 2: Founder insight videos (1× per month)**
- Talking head, 5–10 minutes
- Topics: XM trends, survey design tips, AI in CX
- No need for professional studio — good lighting, iPhone, ring light = fine
- These build personal brand and trust

**Type 3: Customer stories (when you have them)**
- Short documentary: problem → solution → result
- Length: 3–5 minutes
- Most effective sales content that exists

### YouTube SEO
- Title: Put the keyword first. "AI Survey Analysis: How Crystal answers questions your analyst can't"
- Description: First 2 sentences are SEO-critical. Include keywords naturally.
- Tags: "survey analytics", "AI feedback analysis", "Crystal AI", "Xperiq", "experience management"
- Chapters: Add timestamps for each section — improves watch time and findability

### Thumbnail formula
- Bold claim text (3–6 words) in large Manrope font
- Crystal Purple or Xperiq Blue background
- Crystal mark icon in corner
- Example: "Crystal finds this in 30 seconds" over a blurred screenshot

## Platform 4: Instagram

### Why Instagram
Brand building and hiring. Not a direct revenue channel at early stage. Use it to make Xperiq feel real, human, and aspirational.

### What to post

**Format 1: Product screenshots (polished)**
Beautiful app screenshots with branded frames. Show the insight layer, Crystal conversation, survey results.

**Format 2: Team and culture**
Real photos: team at work, office setup, milestone celebrations. These build trust and help with recruiting.

**Format 3: Quote graphics**
Pull quotes from blog posts or LinkedIn posts — designed as 1080×1080px cards in your brand colors.

**Format 4: Reels (short video)**
Repurpose the first 30 seconds of your Crystal demo YouTube videos as Reels. Square crop + captions.

### Posting schedule (Instagram)
- 2–3 posts per week
- Stories: daily (behind-the-scenes, quick polls, product updates)

### Instagram bio link
Use Linktree (free) to host multiple links: website, demo booking, newsletter, YouTube.

## Scheduling Tool

**Buffer** (free tier): Schedule up to 3 channels, 10 posts queued at a time.
Upgrade to Essentials ($6/month) when you need more.

**Alternative:** Later (free for 30 posts/month per profile) — better for Instagram.

### Batch content creation
Spend 2 hours every Monday creating content for the week:
- Write 3 LinkedIn posts
- Write 5 X posts
- Schedule in Buffer
- Film one Crystal demo clip if you have 20 extra minutes

This is the only way to stay consistent without burning out.

## What Not to Do on Social

- **Do not** post the same content verbatim across all platforms — adapt format and length
- **Do not** ignore comments — every unanswered comment signals you don't care
- **Do not** post links in the body of LinkedIn posts (kills reach)
- **Do not** go dark for more than 5 days — consistency beats quality
- **Do not** buy followers — fake followers destroy engagement rate
- **Do not** use social to only announce features — educate 80%, promote 20%

---

*Social media compounds over time. The account with 50 consistent posts crushes the account with one viral post followed by silence. Show up every week, stay in character (confident, clear, human), and the audience will grow.*
