# Xperiq Brand Governance & Legal Protection

**Version 1.0 — June 2026**
**Owner: CEO / Brand Steward**

This document covers how the Xperiq brand is protected, maintained, and controlled — both legally and operationally. Corporations spend millions on brand governance. Startups cannot afford to, but they can afford to be thoughtful. This document is the lightweight version that gets you 90% of the protection at 10% of the cost.

---

## 1. Why Brand Governance Matters

The Xperiq brand is an asset. As you grow, it becomes one of the most valuable assets the company has — more valuable than any single piece of code. Brand governance is how you protect that asset.

Three things that destroy brand value without governance:
1. **Inconsistency** — every team member says something slightly different, and the brand becomes blurry
2. **Dilution** — partners, agencies, and contractors use the logo wrong, mix colors, or use the wrong voice
3. **Infringement** — someone else uses your name or logo (or something confusingly similar) and you have no recourse because you did not file a trademark

This document prevents all three.

## 2. Legal Protection — What to File and When

### 2a. Trademark Registration

A trademark gives you the legal right to use the Xperiq name and logo exclusively in your market. Without it, anyone can use a similar name and you have limited legal recourse.

#### What to file
| Mark | Priority | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| "Xperiq" (word mark) | **File immediately** | Covers the name in all fonts and formats |
| Xperiq logo (design mark) | File when logo is final | Covers the specific logo design |
| "Crystal by Xperiq" | File by Month 3 | Covers the AI sub-brand |
| "Experience + IQ" | File if used as tagline consistently | Lower priority |

#### Where to file
- **US:** USPTO (United States Patent and Trademark Office) — `tmsearch.uspto.gov`
  - Cost: $250–$350 per class per mark (online filing)
  - Timeline: 8–12 months to registration
  - Use an attorney: Clerky, Stripe Atlas, or a referral — $1,500–$3,000 per mark
- **International:** WIPO Madrid Protocol — file in multiple countries at once
  - File this after US registration, when you have international customers
  - Cost: $2,000–$5,000 per country class

#### Classes to file in
Trademarks are filed by "class" (industry category). File in:
- **Class 42** — Software as a service (SaaS); computer technology services (primary)
- **Class 35** — Business intelligence, data analysis, market research services (secondary)
- **Class 9** — Software applications (consider for future)

#### Before filing — run a conflict search
1. Search USPTO TESS: `tmsearch.uspto.gov/bin/gate.exe?f=login&p_lang=english&p_d=trmk`
2. Search Google: "Xperiq" + "trademark" + your industry
3. Search the target countries' trademark databases
If there is a conflict, consult an attorney before proceeding.

### 2d. Copyright

All original content created for Xperiq — blog posts, demo videos, sales materials, the Brand Guide itself — is automatically copyrighted the moment it is created (in the US and most countries). No registration required for basic protection.

**What to do:**
- Add `© 2026 Xperiq Inc. All rights reserved.` to the footer of your website
- Add copyright notice to the footer of all documents, slide decks, and PDFs
- Ensure work-for-hire agreements with freelancers explicitly assign copyright to Xperiq (standard in a contractor agreement — use Clerky or Stripe Atlas templates)

## 3. Operational Brand Governance

### 3a. Brand Approval Process

Anyone creating content that represents Xperiq publicly must follow this process:

#### Tier 1: No approval needed (self-publish)
- Standard social media posts that follow the Brand Guide voice and visuals
- Replies and comments on social media
- Internal Slack messages

#### Tier 2: Manager review (24-hour turnaround)
- Blog posts before publishing
- Email newsletters before sending
- Sales proposals and presentations
- Customer case studies

#### Tier 3: CEO approval (48-hour turnaround)
- Press releases
- New marketing campaigns
- Any co-branded content with partners
- Significant changes to website homepage copy or pricing page
- Any content that makes specific competitive claims
- Any content that references customer data or case studies (check NDA first)

#### Tier 4: CEO + Legal review (72-hour turnaround)
- Changes to the Brand Guide itself
- Changes to logo, colors, or tagline
- Trademark or IP matters
- Any crisis communication statement

### 3b. Partner and Co-branding Guidelines

When Xperiq works with partners — integration partners, resellers, agencies, or event sponsors — the following rules apply.

**What partners CAN do:**
- Use the Xperiq name in text to describe the integration/partnership
- Use the approved Xperiq logo (provided from the official asset folder)
- Say "Powered by Xperiq" or "Integrates with Xperiq" using approved lock-up

**What partners CANNOT do:**
- Modify the Xperiq logo (change colors, distort, add text to the logo)
- Use Xperiq branding to imply endorsement without written permission
- Create their own Xperiq-branded materials without Xperiq review
- Use an outdated logo or outdated brand colors
- Use the Crystal mark separately from the Xperiq brand context (unless agreed)

**Process for new partner co-branding:**
1. Partner submits their proposed co-branded asset to `brand@xperiq.ai`
2. Xperiq reviews within 48 hours
3. Feedback given with required changes
4. Final approval by CEO

### 3d. Brand Asset Management

All brand assets must live in one place, accessible to the whole team. No one should be searching their desktop or emails for the current logo.

**Primary asset storage:** Google Drive shared folder — `Xperiq Brand Assets`
**Backup:** Figma (for editable source files)

**File naming convention:**
`xperiq-[asset-type]-[variant]-[size].[format]`

Examples:
- `xperiq-logo-horizontal-color.svg`
- `xperiq-logo-icon-white.png`
- `xperiq-wordmark-dark.svg`
- `xperiq-social-linkedin-banner.png`

**Version control:**
- When a brand asset is updated, rename old version with `-v1`, `-v2` suffix
- Add a changelog note to the folder with what changed and when
- Notify the team (Slack #brand channel) when a new asset version is available

**Asset access levels:**
| Who | Access level |
|-----|-------------|
| All employees | View + download |
| Brand Designer | Full edit |
| Head of Marketing | View + download |
| External agencies | Download only (specific approved files) |
| Public | None (brand assets are not public by default) |

## 4. Brand Compliance Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing any major piece of Xperiq content.

### Visual compliance
- [ ] Is the logo the current approved version?
- [ ] Are only brand-approved colors used?
- [ ] Is the typography Manrope (headings) and Inter (body)?
- [ ] Is the logo surrounded by adequate clear space?
- [ ] If photography is used, does it match the photography style in `BRAND_STRATEGY.md`?

### Verbal compliance
- [ ] Is the brand name spelled "Xperiq" (capital X, lowercase q)?
- [ ] Is Crystal referred to as "Crystal" or "Crystal by Xperiq" (not "the AI" or "chatbot")?
- [ ] Does the copy avoid the banned phrases from `BRAND_GUIDE.md`?
- [ ] Does the tone match the brand personality (confident, clear, human)?
- [ ] Are all statistics and claims accurate and sourced?

### Legal compliance
- [ ] Is the copyright notice present (for website, documents, PDFs)?
- [ ] Are any competitor names or claims accurate and defensible?
- [ ] Is customer information anonymised or permission granted?
- [ ] Has any required legal review been completed?

## 5. Brand Crisis Playbook

If Xperiq's brand is damaged — by a data incident, bad press, negative viral moment, or employee action — follow this process.

### Severity levels

| Level | Example | Response time | Approver |
|-------|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Low** | One angry tweet, one bad G2 review | 24 hours | Marketing Lead |
| **Medium** | A blog post criticising Xperiq, multiple negative reviews | 4 hours | CEO + Marketing Lead |
| **High** | Data breach, product outage affecting many customers, viral negative story | 1 hour | CEO + Legal |
| **Critical** | Regulatory action, major security incident, existential press story | Immediate | CEO + Board + Legal |

### Response principles (for all levels)
1. **Acknowledge fast.** A 1-hour acknowledgement is always better than a 24-hour silence.
2. **State facts only.** Do not speculate. Do not assign blame. "We are investigating" is a valid first statement.
3. **Own the mistake.** Customers and press respect companies that take responsibility. Defensiveness amplifies the story.
4. **Fix the problem.** Every crisis statement must include what you are doing to resolve it.
5. **Follow up.** Once the crisis is resolved, publish a post-mortem. This rebuilds trust.

### What not to do in a crisis
- Delete critical social media comments (screenshots them first, then you look worse)
- Have multiple team members responding with different messages
- Make legal threats publicly
- Go silent for more than 4 hours during a high/critical incident
