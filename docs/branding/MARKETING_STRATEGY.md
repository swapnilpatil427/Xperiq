# Xperiq Marketing Strategy

**Version 1.0 — June 2026**
**Owner: Marketing Team**

---

## The Marketing Job

Marketing for Xperiq has one job: get the right people to book a demo with Crystal.

Everything else — content, social, ads, SEO — is in service of that one outcome. A great LinkedIn post that doesn't lead to demo interest is entertainment. A boring cold email that books 3 demos is marketing.

---

## 1. Who We Sell To (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary ICP

| | Description |
|--|-------------|
| **Company size** | 50–5,000 employees |
| **Industry** | Technology, Professional Services, Financial Services, Healthcare, Retail, HR Consulting |
| **Geography** | US (primary), UK + Australia (secondary) |
| **Budget signal** | Already paying for surveys (SurveyMonkey, Typeform, Qualtrics) = proven willingness to pay |
| **Pain** | "We collect a lot of feedback but don't know what to do with it" |
| **Win trigger** | Recently hired a CX lead, launched a new product, or had a bad NPS quarter |

### Buyer roles (who signs the check)

| Role | Title | Why they care |
|------|-------|---------------|
| Primary buyer | VP/Director of Customer Experience | Owns NPS, CSAT; needs to show results to leadership |
| Secondary buyer | VP/Director of HR / People | Employee surveys, engagement; needs to prove ROI |
| Secondary buyer | VP/Director of Operations | Efficiency feedback loops; needs to close gaps fast |
| Champion (internal) | CX Analyst, Research Manager | Does the work; wants a better tool; sells it up |
| Economic buyer | CFO / COO | Approves spend; needs to see cost-per-insight vs. alternatives |

### Secondary ICP (smaller, faster deals)

Startups and scale-ups (10–50 employees) where the founder is the buyer. Smaller deal size ($150–$500/mo) but faster to close (days, not months). Good for early MRR and case studies.

### Who NOT to sell to
- Solo freelancers running one-off surveys — lifetime value too low
- Government / public sector — procurement cycles too slow at early stage
- Companies with no existing survey or feedback process — they are not ready for intelligence yet

## 3. Content Strategy

### The 3 content pillars

Every piece of content Xperiq creates fits into one of these three pillars.

**Pillar 1: Experience Intelligence (Thought Leadership)**
Teach the audience how to think about experience data. This is the editorial brand — authoritative, original, research-backed.

Examples:
- "Why most CX programs fail (and what to do about it)"
- "The 4 types of insight every XM platform should give you — but most don't"
- "NPS is not dead. Your analysis of it is."
- "How to turn survey responses into business decisions in under an hour"

**Pillar 2: Crystal AI Demos (Product)**
Show, don't tell. Crystal AI is the best sales pitch — every demo post should show Crystal answering a real question in real time.

Examples:
- Screen recording: "I asked Crystal why our NPS dropped 12 points. Here's what it said."
- GIF: Crystal identifying the top theme in 500 responses in 8 seconds
- Video: "5 questions Crystal answered in one sitting" — real data, live session
- Before/after: "What our analyst took 3 days to find. Crystal found it in 40 seconds."

**Pillar 3: Customer Success Stories (Social Proof)**
Real customers, real results. These are the most powerful pieces of content and the hardest to create — but they close deals.

Examples:
- "How [Company] used Xperiq to reduce customer churn by 18%"
- "What [Company]'s HR team learned from 2 months of employee surveys on Xperiq"
- "From 3-week analysis cycle to same-day insights: [Company]'s story"

### Content cadence

| Format | Frequency | Platform | Pillar |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------|
| LinkedIn post | 3× week | LinkedIn | All 3 |
| X/Twitter post | 5× week | X | Pillar 2 + 1 |
| Blog post | 1× week | xperiq.ai/blog | Pillar 1 |
| Demo video | 2× month | YouTube + LinkedIn | Pillar 2 |
- Case study | 1× month | Website + email | Pillar 3 |
| Email newsletter | 1× week | Beehiiv/Mailchimp | Pillar 1 + 3 |

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Channel priority and why

**1. LinkedIn (primary — B2B growth engine)**
Your buyers (CX leaders, HR leaders, Ops leaders) live on LinkedIn. Organic content here is the highest ROI channel at early stage. No budget required.

Goal: 1,000 followers by Day 90. 5,000 by Month 6.

Tactics:
- Founder posts (personal account) outperform company page posts at early stage. Post from your own profile first.
- Company page content: 3×/week — consistency beats virality
- Comment on posts by industry influencers in XM, CX, HR space
- Use polls to drive engagement ("What's your biggest pain with survey data?")

**2. X / Twitter (awareness + community)**
XM, CX, and HR communities are active on X. Less direct-revenue than LinkedIn but good for brand awareness, journalist attention, and tech audience.

Goal: 500 followers by Day 90.

Tactics:
- Repurpose LinkedIn content (shorter versions)
- Post Crystal demo GIFs — these get strong engagement
- Engage in conversations about AI, XM, CX with 3–5 replies per day

**3. YouTube (trust + SEO)**
Long-form demos and thought leadership build trust that short posts cannot. Also surfaces in Google search results.

Goal: 10 videos published by Day 90. 100 subscribers.

Content: 
- Full Crystal demo walkthroughs (5–10 min)
- Founder video essays on XM topics
- Customer stories (short documentary format eventually)

**4. Instagram (brand awareness)**
Lower conversion than LinkedIn but good for brand building and hiring. Post the same visuals from LinkedIn.

Goal: 200 followers by Day 90.

**5. TikTok (optional, experimental)**
Growing B2B presence. Younger buyers exist here. If you or someone on your team is comfortable on camera, test it. If not, skip for now and revisit at Month 4.

## 6. Paid Advertising

### Month 1: Google Ads (search intent)
Start with search ads because people searching for "AI survey tool" or "Qualtrics alternative" are already in buying mode.

**Campaign structure:**
- Campaign 1: Brand terms ("Xperiq")
- Campaign 2: Competitor alternatives ("Qualtrics alternative", "SurveyMonkey alternative")
- Campaign 3: Category terms ("survey analytics platform", "AI feedback analysis")

**Starting budget:** $500–$1,000/month
**Target CPC:** $3–$8 (SaaS average for this category)
**Goal:** Learn which keywords convert before scaling spend

### Month 2: LinkedIn Ads
LinkedIn is expensive ($8–$15 per click) but highly targeted. Use it for retargeting — showing ads to people who visited your website or interacted with your LinkedIn content.

**Ad types to start with:**
- Single image ads (promote a Crystal demo video)
- Lead Gen forms (capture name + email without leaving LinkedIn)

**Audience targeting:**
- Job title: CX Director, VP Customer Experience, HR Director, VP People, Operations Director
- Company size: 50–5,000 employees
- Industry: Technology, Professional Services, Financial Services

**Starting budget:** $1,000/month (can be profitable with even 2–3 demo bookings)

### Month 3+: Retargeting
Use Google and LinkedIn retargeting to show ads to people who visited the website but did not convert. This is typically 3–5× more cost-efficient than cold targeting.

## 7. Email Marketing

### List building sources (in order of priority)
1. ProductHunt launch signups
2. Blog subscribers (add signup CTA to every post)
3. Webinar registrants
4. Free trial signups
5. LinkedIn lead gen forms

### Email types

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Weekly newsletter | Weekly | Thought leadership, 1 insight + 1 Crystal tip |
| Product update | When shipping | Keep active users informed |
| Onboarding sequence | Automated, 5 emails over 2 weeks | Convert trial to paid |
| Re-engagement | Quarterly | Win back cold leads |

### Newsletter format (keep it simple)
**Subject line formula:** "[One insight about XM]" or "Crystal answered this question..."
**Body structure:**
1. One paragraph on an XM insight (original thinking, 100 words max)
2. One Crystal tip or demo clip link
3. One CTA: "Book a demo" or "Try Xperiq free"

**Tool:** Beehiiv (free up to 2,500 subscribers) or Mailchimp (free up to 500)
