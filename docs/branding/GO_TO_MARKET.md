# Xperiq Go-To-Market Plan

**Version 1.0 — June 2026**
**Horizon: 90 days from brand launch**

---

## The Big Picture

Xperiq is launching as a new brand in the experience management (XM) space. This is a crowded market dominated by Qualtrics, Medallia, and SurveyMonkey — but none of them are AI-native. That is our wedge.

**Our GTM thesis:**
> Lead with Crystal AI. Let the AI demo sell the platform.

Every channel, every conversation, every piece of content should be anchored to one thing: Crystal AI is the fastest way to understand your customer experience. Show it. Don't explain it.

## Day 1–30: Build the Foundation

**Goal:** Everything is ready. The brand exists. The website is live. You are posting.

### Week 1: Core assets
- [ ] Logo commissioned and received (or designed in Figma)
- [ ] Website live at `xperiq.ai` with: homepage, pricing, about, contact
- [ ] Social profiles set up on all 5 platforms with profile image + banner
- [ ] First LinkedIn post: "We're rebranding. Here's why." — founder story, personal

### Week 2: Brand voice live
- [ ] Publish Brand Guide internally (team alignment)
- [ ] Crystal product demo recorded (60-second screen recording)
- [ ] Post demo on LinkedIn and X — no paid spend yet, just organic
- [ ] Set up email newsletter (Beehiiv or Mailchimp, free tier)
- [ ] Send first newsletter to existing contacts: "Xperiq is here"

### Week 3: Outbound begins
- [ ] Define ICP list: 50 target companies (see `SALES_PLAYBOOK.md`)
- [ ] Find 3 warm introductions from your network to those companies
- [ ] LinkedIn connection requests to: HR leaders, CX leaders, Ops leaders at target companies
- [ ] Set up HubSpot CRM (free) and add first 50 leads

### Week 4: Content system
- [ ] Publish first blog post: "Why experience data without AI is half the picture"
- [ ] Set up Buffer for social scheduling (free tier handles 3 platforms)
- [ ] Settle into posting cadence: 3×/week LinkedIn, 5×/week X
- [ ] Review: which post got the most engagement? Double down on that format.

**End of Day 30 checklist:**
- [ ] Website live
- [ ] Social profiles active and posting
- [ ] 50 warm leads in CRM
- [ ] First newsletter sent
- [ ] Demo video exists

## Day 31–60: Build Momentum

**Goal:** You are generating leads. Crystal AI is getting traction. 3 case studies exist.

### Content
- [ ] Publish 2 more blog posts (aim for 1 per week going forward)
- [ ] Create first LinkedIn carousel: "5 things Crystal AI can answer about your survey data"
- [ ] Publish Crystal AI demo on YouTube (longer version, 3–5 minutes)
- [ ] Start collecting testimonials from early users/testers

### Sales
- [ ] Have 20 discovery calls booked (from outbound + inbound from content)
- [ ] Deliver 10 product demos
- [ ] Draft 3 proposals
- [ ] Aim: 50 warm leads → 3 paying customers or 3 pilots in progress
- [ ] Document 3 case studies (even if small — real usage, real result)

### Partnerships
- [ ] Identify 5 potential integration partners (tools your ICP already uses: Slack, Salesforce, Jira, HubSpot, Zendesk)
- [ ] Reach out to 2 potential referral partners (consultants who sell into HR/CX)
- [ ] Consider a co-marketing post with one complementary tool

### Paid advertising — start small
- [ ] Google Ads: search campaigns for "AI survey analytics", "experience management software" ($500 test budget)
- [ ] Measure: cost per click, click-through rate. Do not optimise yet — just learn.

**End of Day 60 checklist:**
- [ ] 3 paying customers or active pilots
- [ ] 3 case studies drafted
- [ ] YouTube channel active
- [ ] Google Ads running
- [ ] 100 leads in CRM

## Day 61–90: Launch Publicly

**Goal:** ProductHunt launch. First press mention. Revenue growing.

### ProductHunt Launch (target: Day 75–80)
- [ ] Claim Xperiq page on ProductHunt (already done in Day 0)
- [ ] Find a hunter with 500+ followers to post it (reach out in ProductHunt communities)
- [ ] Prepare assets: tagline, description, 3 screenshots, demo GIF, video link
- [ ] Line up 30+ people to upvote on launch day (email list, LinkedIn connections, team)
- [ ] Launch on a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday (avoid Mondays and Fridays)
- [ ] Respond to every comment within 2 hours of launch

**ProductHunt goal:** Top 5 of the day = press coverage, backlinks, signups.

### Press
- [ ] Write launch press release (template in `PR_COMMUNICATIONS.md`)
- [ ] Send to 5 target journalists (see `PR_COMMUNICATIONS.md` for list)
- [ ] Pitch TechCrunch, VentureBeat, and 1 industry-specific outlet (CX Today, HR Brew)
- [ ] Even without coverage: post the press release on your website/blog

### Awards & Recognition
- [ ] Submit to G2 (create a G2 profile, ask first 5 customers to leave reviews)
- [ ] Submit to Capterra / GetApp
- [ ] Find 1 startup award to apply for (local chamber, SaaS awards, etc.)

### Revenue target
- [ ] 10 paying customers by Day 90
- [ ] $10,000 MRR (month 3) is a realistic early target depending on pricing tier

**End of Day 90 checklist:**
- [ ] ProductHunt launched (top 10 of the day goal)
- [ ] 1+ press mention
- [ ] 10 paying customers
- [ ] G2 profile with 3+ reviews
- [ ] Reporting cadence established (weekly growth review)

## Weekly Growth Review (ongoing after Day 90)

Run this meeting every Monday, 30 minutes, with your team:

1. **What's working?** (top 3 — double down)
2. **What's not working?** (drop or fix)
3. **Leads this week** — how many came in, from where?
4. **Revenue this week** — new MRR, churn, net new
5. **Content performance** — best post of the week
6. **This week's priority** — one specific goal

Track everything in a simple Google Sheet. Keep it visible to the whole team.
