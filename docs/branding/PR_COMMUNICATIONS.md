## The PR Philosophy

At an early stage, PR is not about getting famous. It is about building a body of credibility — so that when a buyer Googles "Xperiq" before signing a contract, they find press mentions, ProductHunt recognition, G2 reviews, and thought leadership. That library of proof closes deals.

**Priority order for PR:**
1. G2 / Capterra reviews (most trusted by buyers)
2. ProductHunt launch (drives signups + backlinks)
3. Industry press (CX Today, HR Brew) — more reachable than TechCrunch
4. Top-tier tech press (TechCrunch, VentureBeat) — when you have funding news or big data

## 5. ProductHunt Launch Checklist

ProductHunt is the most important single launch event for a B2B SaaS product. A top-5 finish generates: press coverage, backlinks, hundreds of trial signups, and social proof that lasts for years.

### 6 weeks before launch
- [ ] Claim your ProductHunt page and complete the profile
- [ ] Find a hunter with 500+ followers (browse @makers community, ask on Twitter)
- [ ] Agree on the posting date with your hunter
- [ ] Prepare all assets (thumbnail, gallery images, video, tagline)

### 4 weeks before launch
- [ ] Announce the launch date on social media: "We're launching on ProductHunt on [date] — follow our page to get notified"
- [ ] Start building your launch email list: every new signup gets an email asking them to upvote on launch day
- [ ] Prepare your launch day script (how you'll respond to comments)

### 1 week before launch
- [ ] Brief your entire network (investors, friends, customers) — send them the PH link, ask them to upvote on the morning of launch day
- [ ] Write 10 thank-you comment templates for launch day
- [ ] Final check: all PH assets uploaded, video working, tagline tight

### ProductHunt assets needed
- [ ] Thumbnail image (240×240px) — use crystal mark on Xperiq Blue
- [ ] Gallery images (1270×760px, up to 5) — best screenshots, Crystal demo
- [ ] Demo video (60–90 seconds — link to YouTube video)
- [ ] Tagline (60 characters max): "Experience + IQ — AI copilot for your survey data"
- [ ] Description (260 characters): explain Crystal, the insight layers, who it's for

### Launch day
- [ ] Hunter posts at 12:01am PST on launch day (the ProductHunt clock resets at midnight PST)
- [ ] Send the email blast to your list immediately after it goes live
- [ ] Post on all social channels: "We're live on ProductHunt today — every upvote helps!"
- [ ] Respond to every comment within 30 minutes — this signals legitimacy and boosts ranking
- [ ] Do NOT ask people to "upvote" in public posts — say "check us out" or "support us" (PH penalises obvious upvote-begging)
- [ ] Monitor throughout the day; thank top commenters personally

### After launch
- [ ] Collect the final stats (rank, upvotes, comments)
- [ ] Write a post-launch blog post: "What we learned from our ProductHunt launch"
- [ ] Add "Featured on ProductHunt" badge to your website
- [ ] Follow up with every trial signup from launch day within 48 hours

## 6. Spokesperson Guidelines

Until Xperiq has a dedicated communications team, all media interactions go through the founder.

### What to say if a reporter calls
1. Always ask for the reporter's deadline before answering anything
2. "On background" vs "on the record" — agree before you start talking
3. You can always say: "I want to give you an accurate answer — can I call you back in 30 minutes?"
4. Never speculate about competitors, market share, or unannounced features
5. Always bring the conversation back to: Crystal AI, the customer problem, and the "Experience + IQ" positioning

### Topics we speak on authoritatively
- AI in experience management
- Survey design and analysis best practices
- The future of XM (moving beyond dashboards to intelligence)
- Crystal AI capabilities and roadmap
- Customer and employee experience trends

### Topics to avoid
- Specific customer names (without their permission)
- Unannounced funding or acquisition conversations
- Competitive pricing comparisons
- Internal team or company challenges

## 7. Crisis Communication

If something goes wrong (data incident, service outage, bad press), follow this process:

**Step 1 (within 1 hour):** Draft a factual statement of what happened. Do not speculate. Do not blame.

**Step 2 (within 2 hours):** Post on status page, email affected customers directly.

**Step 3 (within 4 hours):** If press picks it up, have a one-paragraph response ready.

**Step 4 (within 24 hours):** Publish a full post-mortem on the website blog.

**What not to do:** Go silent. Silence is interpreted as guilt or incompetence.
