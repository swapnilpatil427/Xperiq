# Xperiq Brand Management Team

**Version 1.0 — June 2026**
**Status: Active**

## Overview

The brand management team is responsible for building, protecting, and evolving the Xperiq brand. At an early stage, this team is lean — one or two people doing the work of ten. As the company grows, roles expand. This document defines who owns what, who approves what, and how the team works together.

**Brand steward:** The final word on any brand decision belongs to the CEO. Every role below advises, creates, and executes — but the brand is ultimately the founder's responsibility until a dedicated Brand Director is hired.

## 2. Roles to Hire (in priority order)

### Role 1: Brand Designer / Visual Identity Lead
**Hire by:** Month 1–2 (or engage as freelancer immediately)
**Why urgent:** Every asset — website, social, sales deck, logo — requires this person. You cannot launch professionally without them.

**Responsibilities:**
- Execute the logo from the brief in `BRAND_GUIDE.md`
- Build all assets in `ASSETS_CHECKLIST.md`
- Maintain the visual identity system (colors, typography, templates)
- Design all marketing collateral, social graphics, and presentation templates
- Own the Figma files for all brand assets
- Ensure visual consistency across all channels

**Where to find:**
- Contra (contra.com) — vetted freelance designers, no platform fee
- Dribbble (dribbble.com/jobs) — portfolio-first hiring
- Toptal — higher cost, highest quality
- LinkedIn search: "Brand Designer SaaS"

**What to look for:** Portfolio showing B2B SaaS brand work. Clean, modern, systems-thinking. Not just pretty pictures — brand systems they've built end to end.

**Budget:**
- Freelance: $3,000–$8,000 for initial brand build (logo + core assets)
- Full-time: $80,000–$120,000/year (hire when revenue supports it)

---

### Role 2: Head of Marketing / Growth Lead
**Hire by:** Month 2–4
**Why urgent:** Marketing is what converts brand awareness into pipeline. Without this person, the founder is also the marketer — unsustainable after Month 3.

**Responsibilities:**
- Owns all marketing channels: content, social, email, paid
- Executes the strategy in `MARKETING_STRATEGY.md`
- Manages the content calendar and posting cadence
- Runs paid ad campaigns (Google, LinkedIn)
- Reports on marketing metrics weekly
- Works with Brand Designer to produce marketing assets
- Manages external agencies and contractors

**What to look for:** B2B SaaS marketing experience. Has grown a company from 0 to first 100 customers. Comfortable being both strategic and hands-on. Has run content + paid + email without a team.

**Budget:**
- Full-time: $90,000–$140,000/year (US)
- Fractional CMO: $5,000–$10,000/month as a bridge hire

---

### Role 3: Content & Social Lead
**Hire by:** Month 3–6
**Why needed:** Once the marketing strategy is defined, someone needs to execute content daily. This is not a junior role — the person writing for Xperiq shapes the brand's public voice.

**Responsibilities:**
- Writes all blog posts, LinkedIn posts, X threads, email newsletters
- Creates Crystal AI demo clips and captions them
- Manages the social media calendar and scheduling (Buffer/Later)
- Interviews customers for case studies
- Repurposes content across platforms

**What to look for:** Strong writer with B2B SaaS experience. Has grown a LinkedIn following or company blog. Understands XM/CX/HR content landscape. Can interview customers and write their stories compellingly.

---

### Role 4: Sales Lead / Account Executive
**Hire by:** Month 1–3
**Why urgent:** Revenue. Someone needs to run the demos, follow up on leads, and close deals while the founder focuses on product and fundraising.

**Responsibilities:**
- Executes the `SALES_PLAYBOOK.md` end to end
- Manages the HubSpot CRM pipeline
- Runs product demos for all qualified leads
- Sends proposals and negotiates contracts
- Collects customer feedback and feeds it back to product
- Represents the Xperiq brand in every sales conversation

**What to look for:** Has sold B2B SaaS at $500–$5,000/month price points. Has run the full sales cycle solo. Understands HR/CX buyer psychology.

---

### Role 5: PR & Communications Lead
**Hire by:** Month 4–6
**Why:** Once you have traction, PR is how you convert it into press coverage, credibility, and awareness. Before traction, the founder handles all PR.

**Responsibilities:**
- Executes the strategy in `PR_COMMUNICATIONS.md`
- Manages journalist relationships and media list
- Writes and distributes press releases
- Manages award submissions and speaking opportunities
- Monitors brand mentions (Google Alerts, Brandwatch)
- Prepares founder for media appearances

**Budget:**
- PR agency retainer: $5,000–$15,000/month (use for ProductHunt launch and major milestones)
- In-house hire: $70,000–$100,000/year

## 4. Brand Decision Rights Matrix

Who approves what, and how fast.

| Decision | Approver | Timeline |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Change to logo, colors, or tagline | CEO (Swapnil) | Reviewed in 48h |
| New marketing campaign | Head of Marketing + CEO | Reviewed in 24h |
| New social post (standard) | Content Lead self-approves | No approval needed |
| Press release | CEO + PR Lead | Reviewed in 24h |
| New product feature name | CEO + Head of Marketing | Reviewed in 48h |
| Co-branding with a partner | CEO | Reviewed in 48h |
| Responding to a crisis | CEO (immediate) | Within 1 hour |
| Changes to pricing / packaging messaging | CEO + Sales Lead | Reviewed in 24h |
| Hiring a new agency or freelancer | Head of Marketing + CEO (for spend >$2,000) | Reviewed in 48h |

**Default rule:** If in doubt, ask the CEO. No piece of external communication goes out representing Xperiq without someone owning it.

## 5. Brand Review Cadence

### Weekly (15 minutes — rolling async)
- Review social media performance (who posts, what performed, what to drop)
- Flag any brand inconsistencies spotted in the wild (wrong logo, wrong tone)
- Quick check: is the brand guide being followed this week?

### Monthly (60-minute meeting)
- Review all marketing and sales content published in the past month
- Check brand health metrics (see `BRAND_STRATEGY.md`)
- Review any external mentions (press, social, review sites)
- Update any brand guidance based on what's working/not working

### Quarterly (half-day)
- Full brand audit: are we still saying the right things to the right people?
- Review competitive landscape — has anything changed in how competitors are positioning?
- Review the Brand Guide — does it need updating?
- Set brand priorities for the next quarter

### Annually
- Full brand health review (awareness, sentiment, share of voice)
- Consider whether brand evolution is needed (not a rebrand — a maturing)
- Update all templates with new screenshots, updated metrics, fresh case studies

## 6. Onboarding New Team Members to the Brand

Every new hire — regardless of role — should complete brand onboarding in their first week.

### New hire brand checklist
- [ ] Read `BRAND_GUIDE.md` — understand who Xperiq is
- [ ] Read `BRAND_STRATEGY.md` — understand the positioning and values
- [ ] Watch the Crystal AI demo video
- [ ] Set up LinkedIn profile with Xperiq as current employer + correct description
- [ ] Update email signature to Xperiq template
- [ ] Add Xperiq logo to all work profiles used externally
- [ ] Review `ASSETS_CHECKLIST.md` — know where all brand assets live
- [ ] Complete a 30-minute brand conversation with Swapnil (first week)

### For external contractors and agencies
Before any external partner creates branded content:
- [ ] Share `BRAND_GUIDE.md` and `BRAND_STRATEGY.md`
- [ ] Share the brand asset folder (logo files, color codes, fonts)
- [ ] Agree on approval workflow (who reviews, what turnaround)
- [ ] Sign NDA if handling unreleased product information
