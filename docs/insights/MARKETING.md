### Circle 1: The Wedge (the easy first 100 logos)

**SaaS B2B companies, 50–500 employees, $5M–$50M ARR, product-led growth motion.**

- Why they hurt: stuck between SurveyMonkey/Typeform (too basic, no analysis) and Qualtrics (too heavy, too expensive)

### Circle 2: The Beachhead (the first $10M ARR)

**Mid-market enterprise (500–5,000 employees, $50M–$500M revenue) — particularly companies disrupted by the Qualtrics/Forsta/InMoment consolidation.**

- Why they hurt: paying $50K–$300K/yr for XM, facing renewal uncertainty, low actual usage of the platform
- Decision-maker: CXO, VP CX, sometimes CTO
- Sales cycle: 2–4 calls, security review, procurement, 6–10 week close
- **The single-sentence pitch:** "Your Qualtrics renewal is up. Their integration is two years out. We replace 80% of what you use for 10% of the cost — with better trust signals."

### Circle 3: The Ambitious (the year-3 ARR)

**Large enterprise (5,000+ employees), particularly verticals being deprioritized in Qualtrics' integration (financial services, hospitality, retail).**

- Why they hurt: vertical-specific roadmaps stalling during integration; Press Ganey customers worried about non-healthcare features
- Decision-maker: SVP Customer Experience, sometimes Chief Marketing Officer
- Sales cycle: 6 months, full RFP, multi-stakeholder
- **The single-sentence pitch:** "We're what XM looks like when AI is the foundation, not the bolt-on — and we're not absorbing four acquisitions."

## 5. Audience-specific narratives

### 5.1 For the VP of Customer Experience

**Headline:** "Stop waiting weeks for what your customers told you yesterday."

**Body:** Your team is drowning in dashboards but starving for action. Legacy XM platforms were built to show you a chart and let you figure out what it means. Experient is built to *tell* you what changed, why it changed, and what to do about it — in plain English, with every claim cited back to real customer quotes. Your team stops being a reporting service. You start running an action program.

**Proof point:** Show the L4 prescriptive card from the UX mockup — "Fixing 'email verification loop' projected to raise NPS +3.2 ±1.8" — with 24 cited quotes and one-click Linear ticket creation.

### 5.2 For the Head of Product

**Headline:** "Insights that don't need a data analyst to translate."

**Body:** You don't have time to learn Stats iQ. You want to know: what's the #1 driver of detractor sentiment this month, what was different last month, and what specific bug is causing it. Experient ranks every driver with bootstrap-confidence importance scores, surfaces the topics that moved week-over-week, and shows you the actual customer quotes. No SQL, no expert mode, no consultant required.

**Proof point:** Show the Voice view with topic clustering and "Refine clusters" inline action — emphasize that the AI's taxonomy is editable, unlike Qualtrics' fixed industry models.

### 5.3 For the Founder / CEO

**Headline:** "The cheapest seat at Qualtrics costs more than your entire Experient program."

**Body:** Qualtrics' typical enterprise ACV is ~$323K/yr. Medallia starts at $20K. We start at $0 and scale to $200/mo for most companies. You pay per credit — the same way you pay for an LLM call — because that's how AI is supposed to be priced. No services org, no six-month implementation, no sales call required.

**Proof point:** Public pricing page. Calculator: "For 1,000 monthly responses with full analysis: $87/mo. With Qualtrics: $5,000+/mo."

### 5.4 For the Engineering Leader (technical buyer)

**Headline:** "Insights as MCP skills. Call them from Claude, your CI, your customer dashboards."

**Body:** Experient is published as four MCP skills. Your AI agents can create surveys, fetch insights, build workflows. Your customer dashboards can embed the same insight cards. Your incident response can correlate NPS drops with deploys, automatically. Every insight is a structured object — not a chart — so it composes with whatever you're already building.

**Proof point:** The 60-second agentic demo from `docs/PRODUCT_PLAN.md`: "Claude creates a product NPS survey, distributes to Slack, gets insights, creates an alert workflow — zero UI clicks."

### 5.5 For the CFO / Procurement (objection handling)

**Headline:** "Same insight categories, 90% less spend, no multi-year lock-in."

**Body:** Compare line items. NPS calculation: same. CSAT: same. Key drivers: same (we use Shapley regression — the gold-standard method most legacy tools approximate). Topic modeling, sentiment, ABSA, anomaly detection, predictive forecasting, prescriptive actions: all present. Pricing: published, predictable, month-to-month. No floor. No services contract.

**Proof point:** Side-by-side feature checklist from [COMPETITIVE.md §6](COMPETITIVE.md) plus a TCO calculator.

## 6. Packaging & pricing

### 6.1 Three published tiers + enterprise

This refines what's in `docs/PRODUCT_PLAN.md` for the Insights surface specifically.

| Tier | Monthly | Responses included | Insights credits | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | 100 / mo | 100 / mo | Trying it, side projects, demos |
| **Starter** | $29 / mo | 1,000 / mo | 1,000 / mo | Solo PMs, small teams |
| **Pro** | $199 / mo | 10,000 / mo | 10,000 / mo (rollover up to 3 months) | Most companies |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Custom | Custom + SSO, SLA, EU/APAC residency | 5K+ employees, regulated |

### 6.2 Insight credits — the unit economics

One credit = one of:

- 1 descriptive insight generated (`score.*`, `voice.topic` label)
- 0.2 of a diagnostic insight (driver, ABSA, segment contrast)
- 0.05 of a predictive insight (Prophet forecast, churn flag)
- 0.02 of a prescriptive insight (uplift estimate)
- 1 NLQ question via Cmd+K
- 1 voice quote retrieval at scale
- 1 export (PDF, Slack message, ticket)

**On Pro tier, 10,000 credits/mo covers a survey with ~500 monthly responses and weekly full regenerates plus daily incremental updates plus ~200 Cmd+K queries per user.** Most teams use <40% of their quota.

### 6.3 The pricing-page narrative

The pricing page is the marketing weapon. Three rules:

1. **Public.** No "contact sales for pricing." This alone eliminates 70% of the legacy XM sales cycle.
2. **Calculator-driven.** "How many responses per month?" slider + "How many users?" + checkbox for AI features → live price. Compare to Qualtrics/Medallia/InMoment with public reference pricing in fine print.
3. **No annual lock-in by default.** Monthly billing. Annual discount (15%) is opt-in, not coerced.

### 6.4 The Land moves

Three classic SaaS land motions, mapped to ICP:

- **Free → Starter (ICP Circle 1)**: PLG via in-product limits. Hit 100 responses → "upgrade to keep your insights live."
- **Starter → Pro (ICP Circle 1+2)**: feature triggers (segment-level drivers, NLQ history, team seats >3).
- **Pro → Enterprise (ICP Circle 2+3)**: SSO, data residency, SOC 2 evidence, custom retention, dedicated CSM, multi-org.

### 6.5 The Expand moves

- **Per-skill upsell**: "Distribution Skill" (SMS, WhatsApp) and "Workflow Skill" (Linear/Jira/Zendesk integrations) are separately priced
- **Multi-survey discounts**: linear pricing breaks down at 10+ active surveys
- **Org-wide rollups**: enterprise feature for multi-team / multi-product views

## 9. Brand voice

### 9.1 Voice principles

- **Plain English, not consultant English.** "NPS dropped 12 points" not "Net Promoter Score experienced statistical regression."
- **Confident, not condescending.** We're correcting an industry, not winning an argument.
- **Cited, not asserted.** When we claim a benchmark, we cite the source. Sets the same standard our product holds.
- **Tactical, not abstract.** Show, don't tell. Every claim has a demo.

### 9.2 Voice anti-patterns

- "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "next-generation" — banned
- "AI-powered" alone (everyone says it) — say *what* the AI does
- "Leading" / "industry-leading" — banned without a citation
- "Trusted by Fortune 500" — say which Fortune 500, with their permission

### 9.3 The 5-word test

If a sentence works as a tweet without context, ship it. Examples:

- "60 seconds to first insight." ✓
- "Every claim, cited to quotes." ✓
- "The AI-era XM platform." ✗ (vague)
- "Cheaper, simpler, more trustworthy than Qualtrics." ✓

## 10. Objection handling

### 10.1 "We already have Qualtrics / Medallia"

**Response:** "Most of our customers also still have Qualtrics — we don't displace, we *augment.* Run Experient on the same surveys for 30 days, free. See what insights it surfaces that yours misses. If it doesn't move your weekly action volume, no harm done. If it does, you'll know what your renewal conversation should be about."

### 10.2 "How can a 30-person company match a 4,000-person platform?"

**Response:** "We don't match — we leapfrog. The 4,000-person platform is the cost of supporting four overlapping codebases. Our codebase is one, AI-native, written in 2025. We don't have a Clarabridge integration to maintain. We don't have a sales org to feed. The advantage of being small and new in this category is not in spite of size, it's *because* of it."

### 10.3 "What about voice / video / advanced statistical methods?"

**Response:** "Voice and video are on the roadmap — text-only first because that's where 95% of value lives. For advanced stats: Shapley regression with bootstrap CIs (gold-standard), Prophet for time series (Meta's open-source), GoEmotions for emotion (Google's research). Same papers, same methods, no proprietary black box. Often better, because we're not maintaining 15-year-old models." Reference [RESEARCH.md](RESEARCH.md).

### 10.4 "What if you go out of business / get acquired?"

**Response:** "Every insight is exportable. Every response is downloadable. Every API is documented. We are not a black box. Compare to your current vendor's data portability story."

### 10.5 "AI hallucinates — how can I trust this?"

**Response:** "Every claim cites two or more real customer quotes. Every number is computed in code, not by the LLM. The LLM writes the prose, the analytics tools compute the math. Click 'Why this insight?' on any card — you see the prompt, the citations, the verifier pass, the audit trail. We don't ask you to trust the LLM. We show you what the LLM wrote *and* what the math says." Reference [RESEARCH.md §7](RESEARCH.md).

### 10.6 "Your enterprise security story?"

**Response:** SOC 2 Type II by [target date in TRACKER]. GDPR compliant. Data residency in US, EU, APAC. SSO via Clerk/Okta. No model training on customer data (LLM calls explicitly opt-out via OpenRouter). Per-org encryption keys (Enterprise tier). Full audit log per insight.

## 13. KPIs & success metrics

### Acquisition

- Free signups / mo (target: 500 by month 3, 5,000 by month 12)
- Conversion free → paid (target: 5%)
- CAC payback (target: <6 months for self-serve, <18 months for enterprise)

### Engagement

- Insights generated per active org / month (proxy for value delivery)
- Cmd+K queries per user / week
- Action conversion rate: % of L4 insights that produce a ticket/workflow

### Retention

- NRR (target: 110% at month 12; 125% by year 3 as expand motions mature)
- Logo retention (target: 95% gross at month 12)
- DAU/MAU of insights consumers (not just creators)

### Brand

- Share of voice in XM-related search vs. Qualtrics/Medallia (track quarterly)
- Net Promoter Score *of our own customers* (target: >50 — the obvious eat-our-own-dogfood metric)
- Inbound demo requests / month
- Direct competitive switches per quarter
