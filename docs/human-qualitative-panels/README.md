## What Is a Human Qualitative Panel?

Every XM platform answers the same two research questions, but almost no platform
answers both in the same place:

- **Quantitative research** (surveys, NPS, CSAT) tells you **WHAT** is happening —
  percentages, scores, trends, distributions.
- **Qualitative research** tells you **WHY** — the motivations, emotions, language,
  and lived experiences behind the numbers.

A **Human Qualitative Panel** is a structured, ongoing program of engagement with
a curated group of real participants — customers, employees, prospects, or users —
who provide rich, open-ended, often multimedia insight over time.

Unlike a one-time survey, a qual panel is a **relationship**:
- Participants are recruited, screened, incentivized, and retained
- They are engaged repeatedly across different questions, stimuli, and sessions
- Their responses are unstructured — conversations, stories, images, video, reactions
- Analysis requires synthesis, not just aggregation

## The Spectrum of Qualitative Methods

| Method | Format | Participants | Duration | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **In-Depth Interview (IDI)** | 1:1 video or phone | 1 | 45–90 min | Deep dive on complex experience |
| **Focus Group** | Moderated group discussion | 6–10 | 90–120 min | Group dynamics, concept reactions |
| **Async Text Community** | Online discussion board | 20–200 | Days–weeks | Breadth + iteration at lower cost |
| **Diary Study** | Prompted self-reports over time | 10–50 | 5–30 days | In-the-moment experience capture |
| **Concept Testing** | Show stimuli, get reactions | 10–30 | 30–60 min | Messaging, design, product ideas |
| **Ethnographic Intercept** | Observe in context | 5–15 | Real-time | Understanding natural behavior |
| **AI-Moderated Focus Group** | Live group + AI facilitation | 10–500 | 60–90 min | Scale qual to large groups |

A modern qual panel platform should support the first four as a minimum.
The fifth and sixth are differentiators. The seventh is the category-defining bet.

## The Problem With Qual Today

Qualitative research is the most powerful insight tool in XM — and the most
broken in practice.

**Cost.** A traditional focus group with a research facility, professional
moderator, recruiting fees, and incentives runs $8,000–$25,000 per session.
Even an IDI program at scale costs $3,000–$6,000 for 10 interviews.

**Time.** Recruiting qualified participants takes 2–4 weeks. Running sessions
takes another week. Transcription, thematic coding, and synthesis takes 2–3 more.
A qual study is a 6–10 week project minimum.

**The analysis bottleneck.** A 2-hour focus group generates 40+ pages of
transcript. Manual thematic coding by a researcher takes 8–15 hours per session.
Most teams skip it entirely — or delegate to a junior analyst who surface-reads.

**Insight rot.** Qual results live in PowerPoint decks that are presented once,
then filed and forgotten. They are never connected to ongoing quant data. There
is no follow-up. The insights decay.

**Disconnection from quant.** Your NPS dropped 7 points. Your qual study from
3 months ago hinted at onboarding problems. No one connected the dots because
the data lives in different systems with different owners.

## What Human Qualitative Panels Are NOT

- A replacement for rigorous quantitative research (qual is directional, not
  statistically significant at N=15)
- An automated system that eliminates the need for a skilled researcher
  (Crystal assists; humans decide)
- A focus group conducted entirely by AI (AI moderation assists a human moderator
  — the relationship requires a human on the other side)
- A data collection tool that works without participant consent and appropriate
  incentivization (respondent trust is the foundation)
