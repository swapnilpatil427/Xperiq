## The Problem We're Solving

Every CX leader we talked to during discovery said some version of the same thing:

"I spend an hour every Monday morning building the same report I built last Monday. I pull the NPS number, check if it went up or down, look at the comment themes, write a three-sentence summary for my VP, and copy it into a Slack message. I have been doing this every single Monday for two years."

This is a solved problem in BI. Amplitude has scheduled deliveries. Looker has scheduled reports. Tableau has subscriptions. Every serious data product ships recurring reporting because it is table stakes for teams that run on regular operating cadences.

XM platforms have been the exception. Qualtrics offers CSV exports on a schedule. Medallia offers PDF chart dumps that require an enterprise contract and a professional services engagement to configure. SurveyMonkey sends a "your survey has new responses" ping with a link to the dashboard.

None of them write the report for you. None of them tell you what changed. None of them recommend what to do next.

That is the gap Xperiq Intelligence Briefings closes.

## Narrative Arc

### The Monday Morning Frame

The launch story anchors to a universal, relatable moment: Monday morning at 8:47am. The CX manager is at their desk. Before their first meeting, they need to know: did NPS go up or down last week? What are customers complaining about? Is there anything my VP needs to know?

Without Xperiq: they open the survey dashboard, pull the numbers, open a spreadsheet, build a comparison, write a summary. 45 minutes gone.

With Intelligence Briefings: the answer was in their inbox at 6am. Crystal already read the data, found the three things that matter, and wrote a briefing they can forward directly to their VP.

### The "Smart Analyst" Frame

The secondary narrative frame positions Crystal as a tireless analyst who reads every response, finds the signal in the noise, and writes a clear briefing — without being asked, every week, at 6am.

This frame resonates with orgs that have a small CX team or no dedicated analyst. The question we answer: "Who is going to turn our survey data into insights we can act on?" The answer: "Crystal already did. Check your inbox."

### The Forwarding Test

Every design and copy decision in this feature should pass the forwarding test: "Would a CX director forward this email to their VP without editing it?" If the answer is no, we have more work to do. This test applies to:
- The Crystal narrative (does it sound like a senior analyst wrote it?)
- The KPI row (are the right numbers shown, in the right order?)
- The recommendations (are they specific enough to act on?)
- The subject line (would you open an email with this subject line?)

## Competitive Positioning

### Qualtrics XM (the incumbent)

**What they offer:** Scheduled report delivery of dashboard widgets as PNG images or CSV extracts. Requires a dashboard to be manually configured first. No AI narrative. No anomaly detection. Enterprise-only for complex delivery configurations.

**Xperiq's differentiation:** Crystal writes the narrative. We don't send a picture of a chart — we send a briefing that explains the chart, interprets the change, and recommends the next action. Setup takes 2 minutes instead of 2 days.

**When Qualtrics wins:** When the buyer wants maximum dashboard customization and has a dedicated BI team to configure everything. We do not compete on configurability; we compete on intelligence and speed to value.

---

### Medallia (the enterprise challenger)

**What they offer:** PDF report delivery in their enterprise tier. Heavily chart-focused (bar charts, trend lines, NPS gauges). Requires professional services to configure delivery rules and templates. Strong on benchmarking data against Medallia's proprietary industry panel.

**Xperiq's differentiation:** Medallia reports are built for the boardroom slide deck. They look impressive but require interpretation. Crystal Briefings are built for the operating manager who needs to act, not present. We ship faster, cost less to configure, and tell you what the charts mean.

**When Medallia wins:** When the buyer needs proprietary industry benchmarks or deep enterprise integration (Salesforce, ServiceNow). We note this openly — we don't have Medallia's benchmark data set. Our counter: Crystal's recommendations are based on your data, not industry averages.

---

### SurveyMonkey / Momentive

**What they offer:** Basic email notifications when surveys receive new responses. A weekly digest email showing response counts. No themes, no NPS analysis, no narrative. No scheduling configuration beyond on/off.

**Xperiq's differentiation:** SurveyMonkey's "email summary" is a notification, not a report. It tells you something happened; it doesn't tell you what it means. Intelligence Briefings are a full analytical product. This is not a close comparison.

**When SurveyMonkey wins:** When the buyer just wants basic survey tooling with minimal overhead. We are not competing with SurveyMonkey for this buyer.

## Pricing & Packaging

Intelligence Briefings are a paid feature. Free-tier users can create one report (in-app delivery only, no email) to experience the value and convert to paid.

| Plan | Reports | Templates | Recipients | Email Delivery | Slack | PDF |
|------|---------|-----------|------------|----------------|-------|-----|
| **Starter (Free)** | 1 | 2 (Weekly NPS, Closeout) | In-app only | No | No | No |
| **Growth** | 5 | All 6 | 10 per report | Yes | No | No |
| **Growth+** | Unlimited | All 6 + custom | 50 per report | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Enterprise** | Unlimited | All 6 + custom + white-label | Unlimited | Yes | Yes | Yes |

**Pricing rationale:**
- Starter gets enough to experience Crystal narrative quality and feel the "aha moment" of seeing their survey data written up
- Growth's 5-report limit is deliberately generous — most single-survey teams never exceed 5 active reports
- Growth+ unlocks Slack (the team channel use case) and PDF (the exec-review use case) — these are the two features that signal enterprise readiness
- Enterprise unlocks white-label: the org can replace "Generated by Crystal AI" with their own branding in the footer

**Upgrade prompts:**
- When a Growth user tries to create a 6th report: "You've reached your 5-report limit on Growth. Upgrade to Growth+ for unlimited reports."
- When a Growth user tries to add Slack delivery: "Slack delivery is available on Growth+ and Enterprise. Upgrade to deliver briefings directly to your team channels."
- When a free user opens the Report Builder: "You're on Starter. Set up one free briefing with in-app delivery, or upgrade to Growth to send to your inbox."
