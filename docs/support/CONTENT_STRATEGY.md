## 1. The Strategic Premise

Support documentation is the highest-ROI marketing surface Experient has, and almost no one treats it that way.

A prospect who types "how to calculate NPS response rate" into Google is not looking for a product demo. They are looking for an answer. They have a specific problem. They are in high-intent mode. If Experient answers that question clearly, specifically, and immediately — with no sales friction — that prospect has now experienced Experient's competence firsthand. They have not read a pitch. They have used the product's intelligence, even before creating an account.

This is the premise: **the support site is a competence demonstration, not a help desk.** Every article that appears in Google search is Experient showing a potential customer what it feels like to have this team in their corner.

The corollary: **a bad support article is an anti-demonstration.** A vague, jargon-heavy, SEO-stuffed article about NPS that doesn't actually explain anything tells that prospect that Experient doesn't know what it's talking about or doesn't care about their time. Both conclusions are fatal for a platform trying to displace incumbents on quality.

### The Three Jobs of Every Article

Every article published at support.experient.ai does three jobs simultaneously. They are always in this order:

1. **Answer the specific question** — completely, specifically, accurately. This is non-negotiable. An article that doesn't answer the question in its title should not exist.
2. **Demonstrate that Experient is the right tool** — not by saying so, but by showing it. A guide that explains NPS calculation naturally shows Experient's survey builder, Crystal's analysis, and the insights dashboard in the process of explaining the concept. Competence is the pitch.
3. **Create a conversion path** — for anonymous users, a CTA to start a free trial embedded in the article's natural conclusion. For authenticated users, a "Try this in Experient" shortcut that opens the relevant product feature directly.

Job 3 never comes at the expense of jobs 1 or 2. An article that sells before it helps is an article that fails at all three jobs.

### The SEO Opportunity (Rafael Diaz)

The XM space has a search opportunity that most incumbents have failed to capture. Queries like "what is NPS," "how to run employee satisfaction survey," "difference between CSAT and CES," and "how to calculate response rate" collectively represent hundreds of thousands of monthly searches. The companies currently ranking for these terms are answering them with 400-word summaries that exist to capture the click, not to deliver value.

Experient's content strategy wins by going deeper. A 2,000-word article on NPS calculation that includes the formula, common mistakes, industry benchmarks, and a worked example with real data will outrank a 400-word article indefinitely — not because of keyword stuffing, but because it is genuinely more useful, earns more time-on-page, more backlinks, and more return visits.

The goal: **own the category-education layer of XM search within 18 months.** When someone is learning about experience management, they should consistently land on Experient content. When they are ready to buy, Experient is already the trusted source.

### What This Is Not

This is not a traditional support knowledge base. It is not a repository of "how do I click button X" articles that only matter to existing customers. It is not a marketing blog dressed up as documentation. It is not AI-generated content stuffed with keywords.

It is a curated body of genuinely useful content — some technical, some conceptual, some step-by-step — that collectively demonstrates Experient's intelligence about experience management and makes every reader slightly better at their job.

## 2. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Experience Management Fundamentals

**Target audience:** CX professionals, HR leaders, and operations managers who are new to structured experience programs, or who want to deepen their understanding of XM concepts. Also includes students, researchers, and consultants. This is primarily a discovery audience — many are not Experient customers yet.

**Search intent:** Educational / informational. Queries include "what is NPS," "how to calculate CSAT," "difference between CES and CSAT," "employee net promoter score explained," "how to run VoC program."

**Conversion path:** Anonymous readers see a "Try this in Experient — free" CTA at the article's natural conclusion. The CTA links to the most contextually appropriate product entry point (e.g., a survey template, the Crystal demo, the NPS dashboard). No friction: one click to a functional demo, no form required.

**Crystal AI angle:** Crystal appears in Fundamentals articles as the tool that operationalizes the concept. An article explaining NPS includes a section: "How Crystal analyzes NPS at scale" — which demonstrates Crystal's theme detection, trend analysis, and response classification on a sample dataset. The Crystal angle is always specific and data-grounded, never "AI magic."

## 3. Article Templates

### Template A: Concept Article (What Is X)

**Purpose:** Explain an XM concept to someone who doesn't know it yet. Primary use: Fundamentals pillar. Secondary use: introductory sections of Survey Design pillar.

**H1 format:** `What Is [Concept]? [Brief clarifying phrase]`  
Examples: "What Is Net Promoter Score? Formula, Calculation, and Benchmarks" / "What Is Customer Effort Score? The Metric That Predicts Churn"

**Meta description formula (150–160 characters):**  
`[Concept] measures [what it measures]. Learn the formula, how to calculate it, industry benchmarks, and how [Experient/Crystal] helps you act on the data.`

**Intro paragraph structure (3 sentences max, first 100 words):**
- Sentence 1: State what the concept is and why it matters — one sentence, no jargon.
- Sentence 2: The key insight most people miss about this concept.
- Sentence 3: What this article will cover (be specific, not generic).

Do not start with "In today's competitive landscape" or any variant. Do not start with a question. State the concept and its significance immediately.

**Body structure:**

```
## What is [Concept]?
One-paragraph plain-English definition. Include the formula if numeric.

## How to calculate [Concept]
Step-by-step calculation with a worked example.
Include: formula, example inputs, example output, interpretation.
[If applicable: include a table of calculation examples at different values]

## [Concept] benchmarks
Industry benchmarks by sector. 
Source: name the source or state "Experient internal data, June 2026."
Do not present benchmarks without attribution.

## What affects [Concept]?
3-5 factors that move the metric. Be specific.

## How Crystal analyzes [Concept]
[Crystal CTA placement: Section 4 of the article body, after establishing credibility]
Explain exactly what Crystal's relevant skill does with this metric.
Include: what inputs Crystal uses, what outputs it produces, an example output.
Link to the relevant Crystal skill article.

## [Concept] vs. [Related Metric]
Comparison table if the concept is frequently confused with another.

## Common mistakes
3-5 specific, actionable mistakes. Not "not measuring enough responses" — 
specify "running an NPS survey with fewer than 50 responses produces 
statistically unreliable scores — margin of error exceeds ±10 points."

## [Try this in Experient]
[ANONYMOUS USER ONLY — conditionally rendered via {{CTA:discovery:anonymous}}]
One-sentence CTA + button. Text: "Build a [concept] survey in Experient — free."
Link to the most relevant template or feature entry point.
[Do not show to authenticated users — they are already customers]
```

**Crystal CTA placement:** Section 4 of body (after concept is established, before benchmarks section ends). Never in the intro. Never as the first section.

**Related articles section (required, end of every article):**
```
## Related
- [Article title] → [doc_key]
- [Article title] → [doc_key]
- [Article title] → [doc_key]  [max 3, chosen by Crystal embedding similarity]
```

**Was this helpful? prompt (required, after related articles):**
```
Was this article helpful?  [Yes]  [No — tell us what's missing]
```
The "No" option opens a free-text field, not a contact form. Response goes to `support_doc_gaps`.

**SEO checklist (internal — verified before publish):**
- [ ] H1 contains the primary keyword exactly as users search it
- [ ] Primary keyword appears in first 100 words of body
- [ ] Meta description is 150–160 characters, written for click-through
- [ ] Article has at least one H2 containing a secondary keyword
- [ ] At least 2 internal links to related articles in the same pillar
- [ ] At least 1 internal link to the relevant Crystal skill article
- [ ] No external links to competitor platforms
- [ ] Structured data: FAQ schema if article contains Q&A sections
- [ ] Images have descriptive alt text
- [ ] Article length: minimum 1,200 words for Fundamentals pillar

- Sentence 1: State what this guide covers and the expected outcome.

Do not explain why surveys are important before explaining how to create one. The reader already knows.

One sentence that acknowledges the problem and states that this article covers the most common causes and solutions. No preamble.

## 4. Auto-Generation Rules for Crystal's Doc-Writer Skill

When the `doc-writer` Crystal skill generates an external support article (as opposed to an internal admin doc), the following rules apply in addition to the standard doc-writer skill constraints. These rules are loaded as part of the skill's system prompt via its `references/EXTERNAL_RULES.md` file.

### 4.1 Performance Claims

**Rule:** Never make performance promises without attached data.

A performance claim is any statement about speed, accuracy, scale, reliability, or uptime. Claims without data are prohibited in external content because they are unverifiable, legally risky, and reduce trust when they turn out to be wrong.

| Prohibited form | Required form |
|----------------|---------------|
| "Crystal is instant" | "Crystal responds in under 3s p50 (measured June 2026)" |
| "Handles any volume" | "Tested on surveys with up to 500,000 responses" |
| "Near-perfect accuracy" | "Theme detection accuracy: 91% on benchmark dataset (EVALS.md v2.3)" |
| "Always available" | "99.5% uptime SLA (Business plan and above)" |
| "Fast export" | "CSV exports complete in under 30s for surveys with up to 50K responses" |

When the doc-writer skill has no performance data available, it omits the performance claim entirely. It does not substitute an unmeasured claim with a vague one.

### 4.2 Human-Readable Intro Requirement

Every article generated for the external support site must begin with a section that a non-technical reader can follow, regardless of the article's technical depth. This applies specifically to API Reference and Crystal Capabilities articles, which have a tendency to open with a schema table.

The intro paragraph must:
- Be written in plain English (Flesch-Kincaid ≥ 60)
- State what the article is about and who it is for
- Contain no code, no schema tables, no JSON
- Be a maximum of 3 sentences

The technical content begins after the intro.

### 4.3 Troubleshooting Articles — Required Escalation Path

Every troubleshooting article auto-generated by doc-writer must include an "If none of these work" escalation section at the end. This section is required and cannot be omitted even if Crystal believes the documented causes cover all cases. The section must include:
- A pointer to the system status page (`support.experient.ai/status`)
- Instructions for opening a support ticket with the exact fields to include
- Expected response times by plan tier

If Crystal does not have current response time data, it uses the following defaults: "Starter: next business day; Growth: 8 hours; Business: 4 hours; Enterprise: 1 hour."

### 4.4 Discovery Articles — Conditional Try-in-Experient CTA

Articles in the Fundamentals and Survey Design pillars (discovery content) must include a "Try this in Experient" CTA. The CTA is conditionally rendered:

- **Anonymous users:** Show the CTA with a "Start free" link to the most contextually relevant product entry point (a specific survey template, the Crystal demo, or the relevant feature page). Placeholder token: `{{CTA:discovery:anonymous}}`
- **Authenticated users:** Replace the acquisition CTA with a "Try this now" deep link that opens the relevant product feature directly, pre-configured for the task described in the article. Placeholder token: `{{CTA:discovery:authenticated}}`
- **No CTA for internal/admin articles.** Discovery CTAs appear only on pillar 1 and pillar 2 content.

The support site frontend resolves these placeholder tokens at render time based on auth state. The doc-writer skill inserts the tokens; it does not resolve them.

### 4.5 Was This Helpful? — Required Footer

Every article published to support.experient.ai must end with:

```
Was this article helpful?
[Yes]  [No — tell us what's missing]
```

The "No" button must open a free-text input (not a contact form, not a redirect to the ticket system). The input submits to `POST /api/support/feedback` which creates a `support_doc_gaps` entry. The response is anonymous unless the user is authenticated.

This is required for all article types without exception. The doc-writer skill appends this as a standard footer token: `{{FOOTER:helpful_prompt}}`. The support site renders the interactive prompt from this token.

After the "Was this helpful?" prompt, Crystal automatically surfaces 2 related article links based on pgvector embedding similarity. These are computed at render time by the support site backend — not curated by the doc-writer skill.

### 4.6 Prohibited Phrases

The following phrases are banned from all external content generated by doc-writer. They sound like marketing copy in a support context, which breaks trust with users who are trying to solve a real problem.

| Prohibited phrase | Why it's banned | Replacement |
|------------------|----------------|-------------|
| "Powerful AI" | Meaningless without specifics | State the specific capability |
| "Revolutionary" | Marketing hyperbole | Describe what changed and how |
| "Industry-leading" | Unverifiable without citation | Cite the third-party source or remove |
| "Best-in-class" | Same as above | Same as above |
| "Seamlessly integrates" | Nothing is seamless when debugging an integration | "Connects to [system] via [method]" |
| "Simply click" | Nothing is simple to someone who is stuck | "Click" (imperative, no qualifier) |
| "Easy to use" | Condescending when a user is struggling | Remove; demonstrate ease by being clear |
| "Intuitive" | Not the user's experience right now | Remove |
| "Game-changing" | Marketing copy | Describe the specific change |
| "Cutting-edge" | Ages immediately | Describe the specific technology |

## 5. Content Freshness SLAs

Content freshness is not a nice-to-have. A support article that is wrong about how a feature works is worse than no article, because it sends users down the wrong path, destroys trust in the site, and increases support ticket volume.

Every article type has a defined freshness SLA. The content engine (see [CONTENT_ENGINE.md](./CONTENT_ENGINE.md)) enforces the technical SLAs automatically via CI. The editorial SLAs are owned by the content team and tracked in the doc health dashboard at `/admin/support/doc-health`.

### API Reference
**SLA:** Updated on every breaking API change. Target lag: < 20 minutes from code push to live doc.

**Mechanism:** Automated via CI. The doc-refresh pipeline detects route and schema changes and triggers doc-writer to regenerate affected articles. A breaking change (annotated `BREAKING` in the commit message or flagged via `!` prefix) triggers an immediate full regeneration of all API articles, not just the changed endpoint.

**Manual review required:** No. Auto-publish if quality score ≥ 0.85.

**What counts as a breaking change:** HTTP method change, path change, required parameter added, required parameter renamed, response shape change, authentication requirement change, rate limit reduction.

### How-To Guides
**SLA:** Quarterly review cycle. Crystal checks whether UI screenshots in how-to articles still match the current application state.

**Mechanism:** Quarterly cron job runs visual comparison between article screenshots and live application screenshots for the same UI state. Mismatches are flagged in the doc health dashboard. The content team reviews and updates within 5 business days of the flag.

**Screenshot verification:** The quarterly cron calls `GET /api/support/screenshot-check` which uses Crystal's visual comparison tool against the current app. Articles with a screenshot mismatch get a `stale_screenshot` flag. They remain published but display: "This guide was last verified [date] — if your screen looks different, let us know."

**Manual review required:** On screenshot mismatch or major feature change.

### Troubleshooting Articles
**SLA:** Updated within 48 hours of every known issue resolution.

**Mechanism:** When a known issue is marked resolved in `support_known_issues`, the system checks which troubleshooting articles reference that issue and triggers a doc-writer update to reflect the resolution. The article's "Is this a known issue?" section is updated automatically.

New troubleshooting articles are created automatically when a known issue is added: doc-writer generates a troubleshooting stub from the known issue description, which a human editor reviews before publish.

**Manual review required:** Yes, for newly created troubleshooting articles. Updates to existing articles on issue resolution are auto-published if quality score ≥ 0.85.

### Concept Articles (Fundamentals Pillar)
**SLA:** Biannual review (every 6 months).

**Mechanism:** Biannual review cron job creates a review task in the content team's queue for all Fundamentals articles. Review checklist: benchmarks still accurate, industry references still valid, Crystal capability descriptions match current SKILL.md output.

**Manual review required:** Yes. Concept articles have the highest brand and accuracy stakes — they represent Experient's expertise on core XM concepts and are the most widely read by non-customers.

### Legal Pages
**SLA:** Annual review plus immediate review on regulatory change.

**Mechanism:** Legal calendar reminder at 12-month intervals. Immediate review triggered by Dr. Kenji Mori's team on: new privacy regulation (GDPR amendments, state privacy laws), Stripe or payment terms changes, AI regulation changes affecting disclosure requirements, FTC guidance changes.

**No auto-generation:** Legal pages are never auto-generated by doc-writer. They are written and reviewed by Dr. Mori's team and published manually. Doc-writer may draft a plain-English summary (see Section 8 on AI Transparency), but the legal text itself requires human authorship and review.

## 6. Content Quality Rubric for External Articles

The existing content engine quality gate (≥ 0.80 to auto-publish) applies to internal admin docs. External support content has higher thresholds because the consequences of a wrong or off-brand article are higher: public visibility, search indexing, brand reputation, and legal exposure.

### Quality Score Thresholds

| Content type | Minimum score to auto-publish | Human review required if |
|-------------|------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Internal admin docs | 0.75 | Score 0.65–0.74 |
| External support articles (How-To, Troubleshooting, API Reference) | 0.85 | Score 0.75–0.84 |
| Discovery / SEO articles (Fundamentals, Survey Design) | 0.90 | Score 0.80–0.89 |
| Any article failing legal risk check | — | Always required |

Articles scoring below the lower threshold for human review are rejected. The existing doc is kept (if any). A `support_doc_gaps` entry is created. The content team is notified via the doc health dashboard.

### Additional Quality Dimensions for External Content

The standard quality score (accuracy, completeness, code example validity, clarity, status) is the base. External articles are scored on four additional dimensions.

**Brand Voice Compliance (0–1 score, minimum 0.80 to publish)**

Evaluated by checking the article against the prohibited phrases list, the voice dimensions in BRAND_VOICE.md, and the tone-by-context matrix. Specific checks:
- No prohibited phrases present (automatic fail if any found)
- Uses second person ("you") throughout
- Intro paragraph is 3 sentences or fewer and contains no jargon
- Technical sections use present tense ("returns" not "will return")
- No marketing language in support context

The brand voice check is run by a separate LLM judge that scores each dimension 0–1 and returns a composite. A score below 0.80 blocks publish regardless of the base quality score.

**Legal Risk Score (0–1, must be < 0.20 to auto-publish)**

Evaluated by checking for: unsubstantiated performance claims, implied warranties, statements that could constitute legal advice, privacy claims that contradict the current privacy policy, AI capability claims not backed by EVALS.md data.

A legal risk score ≥ 0.20 routes the article to Dr. Mori's team for review before publish. A legal risk score ≥ 0.40 blocks publish entirely until legal review clears it.

**SEO Completeness**

Mechanical check against the article type's SEO checklist (see Section 3 templates). Scores 1 point per completed item. Minimum passing score: 8/10 items complete. Missing items are reported in the doc health dashboard.

Specific required items for all external articles:
- Title tag (H1) contains primary keyword
- Meta description: 150–160 characters
- Primary keyword in first 100 words
- Minimum 2 internal links
- No broken links
- Alt text on all images
- Structured data schema (FAQ, HowTo, or Article as appropriate)

**Readability (Flesch-Kincaid ≥ 60 for non-technical articles)**

Non-technical articles (Fundamentals, How-To guides, Troubleshooting): minimum Flesch-Kincaid score of 60 (accessible to general adult readers).

Technical articles (API Reference, Crystal Capabilities): minimum score of 40 (acceptable for technical audience).

The readability check is applied to the article body, excluding code blocks. A readability score below threshold triggers a doc-writer revision pass with an instruction to shorten sentences and reduce passive voice.

## 7. Internal Linking Strategy

Internal linking serves two purposes simultaneously: it helps users find related content, and it builds topical authority in Google's index. A site that links its NPS articles together, from pillar to cluster and back, tells search engines that Experient is an authoritative source on NPS — not a site with one NPS article.

### Linking Rules

**Every article must link to 2–3 related articles.** No exceptions. The links appear in the "Related" section at the end of the article (see templates). These are not manually curated — Crystal computes them at render time using pgvector embedding similarity against the `support_docs` table.

**Pillar pages link to all cluster articles.** Each pillar has a pillar page (e.g., `/crystal/overview`, `/api/overview`) that links to every article in that pillar. Pillar pages are the hub; cluster articles are the spokes.

**Cluster articles link back to the pillar page.** The first section of every cluster article includes a contextual link to the pillar page. Example: the NPS Calculation article links to "See all Experience Management Fundamentals."

**Concept articles link to How-To articles.** An article explaining NPS links to "How to Create an NPS Survey in Experient." An article explaining Crystal's theme detection links to "How to Use Crystal to Analyze Open-Ended Responses." This is the primary conversion path: understand the concept, then use the tool.

**How-To articles link to API Reference.** Every platform how-to article links to the relevant API endpoint documentation at the bottom: "Developers: see the [API Reference] to automate this task."

**Troubleshooting articles link to How-To articles.** A troubleshooting article about export failures links to the Export How-To guide ("If you're new to exporting data, start here").

**API Reference articles link to Crystal API articles.** If a REST endpoint has a Crystal skill equivalent, the API reference article links to the Crystal API article: "Crystal can call this endpoint autonomously — see Crystal API Reference."

### What Not to Link

**Never link to competitor names or platforms.** No mentions of Qualtrics, SurveyMonkey, Medallia, or any competitor in external content. If a comparison is needed (e.g., migrating from another platform), use the generic phrase "another XM platform."

**Never link to external support sites.** External links in support articles should be reserved for primary sources: academic references, standards bodies (ISO, ANSI), industry associations. Never link to a competitor's documentation as a reference.

**Never use "click here" as link text.** Link text must describe the destination: "NPS Calculation Guide" not "click here for more information."

## 8. The AI Transparency Policy

Experient uses Crystal's `doc-writer` skill to auto-generate significant portions of support.experient.ai. This is disclosed, not hidden. The AI Transparency Policy exists for two reasons: regulatory compliance (FTC guidelines on AI-generated content) and user trust (users who know AI wrote the article can calibrate their trust appropriately).

### 8.1 AI-Drafted Badge

Every article where the initial draft was generated by Crystal's doc-writer skill displays an "AI-drafted" badge. The badge appears:
- In the article metadata section (below the H1, above the first paragraph)
- In the article's structured data (`author` field: `"Experient Crystal AI"`)

Badge format:
```
AI-drafted  •  Human-reviewed on [date]  •  [Flag inaccuracy]
```

The badge is not hidden, not in footnote text, and not buried in the footer. It is visible at article load without scrolling.

### 8.2 Human Review Standard

The "Human-reviewed on [date]" timestamp means the following checks were completed by a human editor after Crystal's initial generation:

1. **Tone check:** Article matches brand voice guidelines (BRAND_VOICE.md). Prohibited phrases removed. Second person throughout. No marketing language.
2. **Fact check:** All stated capabilities match current SKILL.md files and product behavior. All benchmarks are sourced. No invented features.
3. **Legal scan:** Article reviewed against the legal risk checklist. No implied warranties, no unsubstantiated performance claims, no privacy policy contradictions.
4. **Link verification:** All internal links resolve. No broken links to deprecated pages.

The human reviewer signs off on the article in the admin dashboard. Their identity is not public, but the review date is. If the article is subsequently auto-updated by CI (e.g., an API reference article updated after a schema change), the human-reviewed date is reset and the article requires re-review before the badge updates.

### 8.3 Flagging Inaccurate AI-Generated Content

The "Flag inaccuracy" link in the AI-drafted badge opens a modal with a free-text field. The user describes the specific inaccuracy. The report is submitted to `POST /api/support/feedback` with `type: "ai_inaccuracy"`. This creates a priority `support_doc_gaps` entry that is reviewed by the content team within 24 hours (regardless of plan tier — inaccuracy reports are always high priority).

The article is not taken down on flag. It remains live while the content team investigates. If the investigation confirms an inaccuracy, the article is updated or marked `[Under Review]` while a corrected version is prepared.

### 8.4 Data Crystal Uses to Generate Articles

Crystal's doc-writer skill operates on the following data sources:

- Route files and Zod schemas from the Experient codebase (source of truth for API docs)
- SKILL.md files from `crystalos/skills/*/SKILL.md` (source of truth for Crystal capabilities)
- TRACKER.md status entries (source of truth for feature availability)
- EVALS.md accuracy data (source of truth for Crystal performance claims)
- Existing support articles (to preserve stable sections when updating)

Crystal's doc-writer skill does **not** use:
- Individual user data or survey responses
- Organization-specific data
- Any personally identifiable information
- Conversation history from Crystal support interactions (those are anonymized before being used as training signal)

This is stated explicitly in the AI transparency section of the support site's Privacy Notice: "When Crystal generates support articles, it uses only Experient's product code and documentation as its source. It does not use your organization's data."
