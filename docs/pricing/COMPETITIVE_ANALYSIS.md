# XM Platform Competitive Pricing Analysis

**Prepared:** June 2026  
**Purpose:** Inform Experient pricing strategy — understand where incumbents are weak and where we attack

---

## Executive Summary

The XM market has a structural gap: **incumbent platforms are smart but punishingly expensive**; **self-serve tools are cheap but dumb.** The mid-market segment ($10K–$75K/year budget) is chronically underserved. Experient enters as the first AI-native XM platform with transparent usage-based pricing — directly targeting the segment incumbents cannot serve without destroying their margin structure.

**Key opportunity:** Medallia transferred to creditors in April 2026 (Thoma Bravo debt restructuring, $5.1B equity wiped). Enterprise accounts are actively evaluating alternatives. This is a 12–18 month displacement window.

---

## 1. Qualtrics XM

### Pricing Model
- Quote-based, 100% sales-led — no public pricing, no self-serve
- Meter: interaction volume (responses collected) + named user seats + optional analytics modules
- Mandatory 3–5 year enterprise contracts
- 5%+ annual price escalation at every renewal

### Contract Sizes
| Segment | Typical ACV | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| SMB / individual CoreXM | $1,500–$5,000/year | Small org totals $6,500–$15,000 |
| Mid-market | $15,000–$75,000/year | Median buyer: $28,533/year (Vendr 2026) |
| Enterprise | $323,532/year avg | Large accounts: $500K–$1M+ |
| Implementation fee | $5,000–$20,000+ | Year 1 only, mandatory |

### What's Included vs. Add-On
- **Base CoreXM:** Survey creation + basic reporting only
- **Extra cost:** Stats iQ (advanced stats), Text iQ (NLP/sentiment), Predict iQ (churn prediction), XM Discover (conversational intelligence), SMS distribution, HIPAA, SSO, multi-language, Panels/XM Directory
- Each add-on is a separate contract line — typical enterprise customer has 4–7 add-ons

### AI Features and Pricing
- iQ suite: Stats iQ, Text iQ, Predict iQ — all sold as add-ons above base
- XM Discover (Clarabridge acquisition) — enterprise-only, $200K–$500K+ separately
- "AI is included" marketing claim applies to basic summaries only; actionable AI layer is always a upsell

### Customer Pain Points (G2, Reddit r/qualtrics, BBB, Trustpilot)
- **Trustpilot: 1.2/5** — 67% one-star reviews; top complaints: poor support, product failures
- **BBB: 37 complaints** in 3 years — billing, unresponsive support
- G2 recurring: "overly complicated," "not intuitive," "very expensive," "new features always cost extra"
- Annual escalation regardless of usage — no discount for loyalty
- Deployment: weeks to months to launch a campaign; requires internal "survey engineers"
- **Overage trap:** commit 10K responses at $50K; collect 25K → overage fees add $75K (150% budget overrun)

### Attack Vectors for Experient
- Transparent pricing vs opaque negotiation
- No minimum commitment for mid-market
- Crystal AI included in base plan, not a $200K add-on
- 30-minute onboarding vs 6-month implementation
- Closed-loop action management as a core feature, not a professional services engagement

## 2. Medallia

### Pricing Model
- **Experience Data Record (EDR)** consumption model — every customer-company interaction = 1 EDR
- Annual volume tiers for base price; each module (digital, contact center, EE, text analytics) separately licensed
- Professional services embedded in every meaningful configuration

### Contract Sizes
| Segment | Typical ACV |
|---|---|
| SMB | $56,681/year (Spendhound 2026) |
| Enterprise | $521,520/year avg |
| Implementation (year 1) | $50,000–$200,000+ |
| Total year-3 TCO | $700K–$1M+ |

### Customer Pain Points
- Platform described as "overwhelming" and "clunky" — even internal navigation requires training
- 6–12 months to first actionable insight (longest time-to-value in category)
- Every configuration change requires paid services engagement
- **Vendor survival risk:** Thoma Bravo transferred Medallia to creditors, April 2026, wiping $5.1B equity — enterprise buyers are factoring this into renewal decisions

### Attack Vector: Medallia Displacement Play
- Target Medallia's $500K+/year enterprise accounts who are actively evaluating alternatives
- Offer 6-month data migration + onboarding guarantee
- Price at 40–60% of Medallia TCO with equivalent analytical depth
- Emphasize Experient's financial health (credit-based = cash-flow positive from day 1)

### SurveyMonkey Enterprise

- 100,000 responses/year included
- **Ceiling:** Data collection only — no intelligence layer

**Competitor position:** Both tools are commoditized. Experient doesn't compete with them directly; they represent the tool companies' customers outgrow.

## 6. AI-Native Challengers

### Hotjar / Contentsquare
- VoC surveys: $99/month, 500 responses — post-acquisition unbundled
- AI Sense feature for summaries — Growth tier and above
- Not a full XM platform: heatmaps + micro-surveys only

### Qualaroo
- Starts at $0; paid from $19–$69/month for in-product micro-surveys
- Single-function tool, not an XM platform

**Pattern:** Most "AI-native challengers" are single-function tools. None provide end-to-end XM — survey design, collection, text analytics, driver analysis, action management — with AI across all layers.

## 8. Customer Willingness-to-Pay Signals

### What Causes Deals to Stall
- Any price above $50K/year triggers CFO sign-off at most mid-market companies
- Implementation timelines of 6+ months kill ROI calculations
- Per-seat minimums force over-purchasing
- Hidden overage clauses discovered mid-year destroy renewal trust

### Features That Justify Premium Pricing
- **Closed-loop action management** — buyers pay significantly more when insights translate into tracked, accountable actions
- **Predictive analytics tied to revenue outcomes** — proven ROI on churn reduction
- **Text analytics at scale** — AI that reads open-ended responses and surfaces themes is the #1 feature mid-market cannot build themselves
- **Speed to insight** — same-day AI-generated analysis vs weeks of consultant work

## 9. Vulnerabilities in Incumbent Pricing

### Minimum Commitment Trap
Both Qualtrics and Medallia require multi-year contracts with seat floors and response volume commitments. Underestimation causes overages; over-commitment pays for unused capacity. A lose-lose that breeds renewal resentment.

**Experient answer:** Flat, transparent plans with a generous bundled allowance and a spend cap on by default. A $299/month plan the customer can start (and cancel) themselves — with a known ceiling — is far more palatable than a $100K/year upfront multi-year commitment.

### Hidden Fee Accumulation
Qualtrics: 5%+ annual escalation + add-on modules + implementation + professional services. Total year-3 cost for a mid-market customer can be 2–3x what they signed.

**Experient answer:** All-in transparent pricing. One number that includes AI, analytics, and implementation support. No hidden fees. Price change requires 90-day notice.

### The Insight-to-Action Gap
30–40% of CX insights collected result in zero organizational action. Qualtrics and Medallia deliver dashboards; they do not deliver decisions.

**Experient answer:** Crystal proposes actions; humans confirm; outcomes are measured. The closed-loop model is the product, not a feature. This justifies pricing based on provable ROI, not seat counts.

### Medallia's Existential Risk
April 2026: Medallia transferred to creditors. Enterprise buyers are evaluating alternatives. This 12–18 month window is Experient's highest-value displacement opportunity.
